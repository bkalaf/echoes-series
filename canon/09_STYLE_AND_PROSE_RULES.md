# Style and Prose Rules

This file defines drafting behavior for `Echoes of Eidolon` manuscripts.

## General Drafting Rules

- Preserve canon before improving prose.
- Do not simplify a chapter by cutting required mechanics.
- Do not invent new cities, ships, Witnesses, rewards, or family relationships without instruction.
- Keep emotional turns attached to action, not abstract explanation.
- Dialogue should carry conflict, concealment, pressure, or relationship movement.
- Avoid making Witness scenes generic boss fights.
- Avoid over-explaining mysteries before their reveal window.

## Chapter Reconstruction Rules

For L4 Reconstruction chapters:

- Preserve the exact scene skeleton.
- Preserve beat order.
- Preserve section breaks.
- Preserve who discovers each clue.
- Preserve who asks each important question.
- Preserve the chapter’s final image / handoff.
- Dialogue can change unless marked verbatim.
- Prose can tighten if the same beat remains intact.

## Horizontal Breaks

Horizontal breaks are visible as:

```md
---
```

They are not automatically subchapter titles. Internal labels may exist in outlines, but should not appear in manuscript unless explicitly requested.

## Dear Reader Voice

Dear Reader interludes may interrupt the story directly.

They should:

- identify a thematic question;
- use fictionalized framing for real-world reference;
- return clearly to Eidolon;
- include a comedic, cutting, or emotionally clarifying exit;
- avoid becoming detached essays.

## Witness Scene Rules

A Witness scene should include:

- domain pressure;
- emotional test;
- sensory identity tied to the Witness form;
- refusal to explain too directly;
- riddle / memory / moral pressure;
- a defeat condition that requires the companion to embody the book feat.

## Prose Bias

Prefer:

- concrete action;
- precise emotional pressure;
- scene-level causality;
- visible objects and gestures;
- dialogue with subtext;
- ending images that hand off cleanly.

Avoid:

- generic fantasy awe;
- repeated exposition;
- flattening political parallels into direct lectures outside Dear Reader chapters;
- resolving tension too early;
- turning every character into the same voice.

## Verification Before Output

Before presenting a chapter draft, check:

- Does it obey the assigned detail level?
- Does it preserve the latest correction?
- Does it preserve all hard canon?
- Does it maintain the intended section breaks?
- Does the chapter ending still point to the next chapter?
- Did any old/superseded names slip in?
