# City-State Founding Triads

## Canon Status

Active canon authority for the original 24 city-state structure and the later 72-city split.

This file formalizes the founding triads planted by the First Mirror / Book 14 council material. It supersedes older city-state grouping tables wherever the question is the original 24-to-72 civic structure.

## Hard Locks

- **Aquila Matara is not part of the original 72-city split.** It was constructed separately as the federal governing capital after the original city-state structure existed.
- The atlas city positions do **not** move for this reassignment.
- The original 24 city-states each began as a triad of:
  - one Human city;
  - one Mythos city;
  - one Beastfolk city.
- The **Original Capital** is always the Human city.
- The **Current Capital** is the present-day dominant civic capital after the triad split and power shifted.
- **Khembal is specifically South African** as of the Book 1 city-allegiance lock.
- **Addie is from Qasr Siroth**, the Human city / current capital of the Saffron Locks triad.
- Mother initially centered the human cities because she assumed they would act as stable civic anchors for mixed peoples.
- Mother grouped Mythos and Beastfolk around humans by presentation logic: what peoples outwardly present as, what they keep hidden, and how culture reads the body.
- Mother did **not** fully anticipate how strongly the three groups would split into separate civic identities.
- Over time, each founding triad separated into three distinct cities. In many regions, the Mythos or Beastfolk city later overshadowed the original Human capital.
- Book 1 should plant this as a mystery, not explain the entire 24-to-72 structure at once.

## Narrative Function

The Book 1 discovery is not that Pollyr Fen moved city-states. The discovery is deeper: the original city-states were all human-led triads, and the present-day capital structure records which culture later became dominant.

This creates two parallel Book 1 investigations:

1. **The civic-history thread:** why the 24 founding city-states became 72 cities, and why the capitals changed.
2. **The atrocity/reward thread:** Bountiful Dam, river diversion, the frog-generation genocide, and Ariadne's Thread.

The First Mirror plants the 24-city-state clue. Luminthalas makes the clue actionable by exposing that the current capital is not always the original capital.

## Sakura Branch / Book 2 Seed

The original Sakura Branch logic should be anchored near **Awanate**, not Vyrath'Kai.

Reason:

- Vyrath'Kai is volcanic island territory and cannot naturally support the Sakura forest.
- Dragonkin are exiled from Awanate and cannot safely keep the original branch there.
- The Sakura Branch therefore has to be planted and tended elsewhere by people who can preserve what the dragonkin cannot keep.
- This supports Gregory's Book 2 arc: the branch is not just a reward object, but proof of exile, displacement, stewardship, and beauty surviving away from its rightful people.

## Book 1 Use

Book 1 should reveal this structure gradually:

1. Addie sees the twenty-four-city-state clue in the First Mirror.
2. The companions go to Luminthalas for Brynn and discover the first evidence that the present-day capital is not always the founding capital.
3. The Luminthalas / Khembal / Shyrenveil triad becomes the first usable example; Khembal is specifically South African.
4. Addie’s Qasr Siroth origin should be preserved quietly in Book 1 and becomes important to allegiance-chain / faction-assignment logic.
5. While researching Bountiful Dam and Pollyr Fen, the group realizes civic history and atrocity history are tangled.
6. The key mystery becomes: why did Mother build human-led triads, and why did the triads split so dramatically?

Do not let Book 1 fully answer the Mythos/Beastfolk split. It should plant the seed for later books.
