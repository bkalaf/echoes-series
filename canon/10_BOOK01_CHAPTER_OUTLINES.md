# Chapter Detail Index

## B01.000 — Prologue: The Mother Who Started the War

### Function

Establish the reader-facing myth/science frame; introduce Mother’s time limit; show Mother finding Brickett; seed the number-pattern solution; reveal that compassion plus a loophole starts the war.

### Cast

Narrator / Dear Reader voice; Mother / Dianne; sleeping Brickett H.; background astronomers.

### Core Beats

1. Narrator frames the story as true and false at the same time.
2. Mother is introduced as ancient, tired, grandmotherly, and running out of time.
3. Skyrend Peak slums are established two days before summer solstice.
4. Mother searches for a forgotten child rather than a king or champion.
5. Mother finds Brickett asleep in a rotting warehouse.
6. Mother leaves the backpack and sealed scroll.
7. Mother creates the blanket from compassion, outside the strict plan.
8. Narrator introduces the number sequence: 3, 9, 18, 24, 72.
9. Mother starts a war through backpack, scroll, blanket, and loophole.

### Continuity Notes

- Brickett is now treated as **14** in current character canon; older references to 16 must be corrected in prose passes.
- Mother’s spontaneous blanket is a key moral seed: compassion modifies duty.
- Check older “24 sovereign territories” language against current one-nation / twenty-four city-state canon.
