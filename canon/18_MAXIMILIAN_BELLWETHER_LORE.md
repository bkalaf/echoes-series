# Maximilian T. Bellwether Lore Lock

## Status

Canon support file for Max / Maximilian T. Bellwether. This file expands the compact row in `canon/05_CHARACTERS.md` and should be used when generating Mae-estate chapters, later Mae memory/vision sequences, and Max dialogue.

## Core Identity

- Full formal name: **Maximilian T. Bellwether**.
- Nickname: **Max**.
- Species / kin: satyr.
- Public role: Mae estate head butler / household manager / cook's assistant / accountant / unofficial therapist / whatever Mae decides.
- Private role: Mae's oldest friend, survivor witness, and the one person in the household who can scold Mae directly without losing his place.

## Mae's Name-Use Pattern

Mae uses his names as emotional tells:

| Mae's form | When she uses it |
|---|---|
| **Bellwether** | When annoyed, irritated, or deflecting emotion. |
| **Maximilian** | When performing threatening politeness, usually as a warning that she is close to snapping. |
| **Max** | Only when emotionally sincere, privately vulnerable, or strategically urgent. |

Use this deliberately in prose. Do not treat `Max`, `Bellwether`, and `Maximilian` as interchangeable.

## Hidden Descent

Max is a secret descendant of **Geoffrik T. Vayne**.

This should remain hidden for now. Do not reveal it casually in Book 1 prose. The public and most companions should know only the Bellwether identity.

## Flight From Aes Sidhal

Max's parents fled **Aes Sidhal** for an unknown reason shortly before a purge.

Max was a child. What he remembers clearly:

- being taken away quickly;
- adults refusing to explain enough;
- his name being changed;
- danger around the old name;
- the sense that everyone was lying to keep him alive.

The specific Vayne connection and the reasons for the flight should remain delayed truth.

## Public School Bond With Mae

Max met Mae in public school after Mae's adopted mother refused to pay for private school.

This matters because both of them were displaced from the lives they were supposed to have:

- Max was hiding under a new name after his family fled Aes Sidhal.
- Mae was being punished / controlled by an adopted mother and household that treated education as a weapon.

Their friendship is not servant/noble first. It is childhood survival first.

## Satyr Deportation Memory

Roughly thirty years before Book 1, Dovren moved to cancel the immigration status of satyrs and send them back to Aes Sidhal.

The ruling leadership in Aes Sidhal had allied with Dovren to recover political dissidents. Returned satyrs were being registered at the Portalport and executed in the street almost immediately after arrival.

This should become a later vision/memory sequence, likely chosen by the Architects/Witness structure rather than volunteered by Mae. Mae watches and realizes: **this is my memory**.

## The Bellwether Name Origin

During the deportation crisis, young Mae manipulated her adopted mother and adopted brother into hiring Max's parents as servants so the family would be protected inside the Van'kareth household.

When asked their name, Mae had not prepared one and invented **Bellwether** on the spot.

Required memory-shape beats:

1. Mae is trying to save Max and his parents without admitting what she is doing.
2. Her adopted mother / aunt and adopted brother ask what the family's name is.
3. Mae stammers because she did not think that far ahead.
4. She says: **Bellwether**.
5. The adults note that it is an odd name.
6. Mae answers: **"Well, they're odd people."**
7. The adopted brother finds it amusing and suggests installing bells everywhere so the family can be summoned without anyone learning their names.
8. This becomes the origin of the manor's service-bell system.

## The Bell System

Mae's adopted family installed service bells throughout the house as a cruel joke on the invented Bellwether name.

When Mae later took over the Aquila Matara manor after being sent there to work with the Conclave — effectively exiled from Nox Vaelor without formal exile — she ended the practice.

Current manor state:

- old service bells remain in rooms and corridors;
- most are taped down, wrapped in cloth, disabled, removed from clappers, or otherwise prevented from ringing;
- staff do not use them;
- Mae refuses to let the house operate through bell-summoning.

## Book 1 Seeding

Seed the bell system in stages:

1. **First manor night / `01_08`** — Addie notices a muted service bell in a room, taped down or wrapped in cloth, and files it as odd.
2. **Second manor stay / `01_09` or next manor movement** — Addie notices similar disabled bells in more rooms and realizes it is a pattern, not one broken object.
3. **Later manor pressure chapter / `01_22`** — Matthieu or Carrio asks what is with all the bells. Max laughs. Mae says not to get him started, then explains the family used the bells to call servants and that she stopped the practice when she took over the house.

Do not reveal the deportation / Bellwether-name origin in Book 1 unless specifically approved. Book 1 should only show that the bells matter and that Mae deliberately disabled the practice.

## Max Can Scold Mae

Max is allowed to confront Mae in ways other staff cannot.

In the late red-beacon pressure sequence, after Mae isolates in her wing and takes out her anger on him, Max should be the one who can call her on it. He is not merely a servant being mistreated; he is a friend strong enough to absorb the first blow and throw truth back.

This should make Mae sharper, not crueler: she snaps at him because he is safe enough to snap at, and he refuses to let her hide behind rank.

## Later Vision Function

The deportation memory should eventually do three jobs:

1. deepen Mae and Max's bond for the reader;
2. show Dovren's state violence against satyrs / political dissidents;
3. connect companion wounds to Witness/Architect history by showing that Mae's private memory belongs to the same system of coerced survival, registration, deportation, and names as the larger myth.
