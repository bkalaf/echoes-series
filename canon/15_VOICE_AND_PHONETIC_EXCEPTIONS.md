# Voice and Phonetic Spelling Exceptions

This file is the canon authority for characters whose speech intentionally uses limited phonetic spelling.

## Global Rule

Phonetic spelling is **not** the default for accents in *Echoes of Eidolon*.

For most characters, accent and cultural influence should be carried through:

- rhythm
- diction
- sentence structure
- formality level
- idiom pressure
- word choice
- class / profession markers

Do **not** use heavy dialect, joke spelling, eye dialect, or caricature unless a specific character is explicitly listed below.

## Explicit Exceptions

### Brynn H. Stonevain

- Status: **Explicit phonetic-spelling exception.**
- Applies to: Brynn's spoken dialogue in scenes where her accent is active.
- Voice Basis: Heavy, clipped, gravelly dwarven / Scots-coded accent.
- Default Behavior: Brynn's accent should be represented directly in dialogue with controlled phonetic spelling.
- Acceptable tools:
  - contractions such as `ye`, `yer`, `ye're`, `o'`, `nae`, `cannae`, `dinnae`, `ain't` when they fit her sentence rhythm
  - dropped final consonants in restrained form, especially `-in'` rather than every possible dropped sound
  - blunt syntax and compressed phrasing
  - hard practical word choice
- Avoid:
  - making every word phonetic
  - turning her into comic-relief dialect
  - using accent density so heavy that readers cannot parse the line
  - making her sound less intelligent because of the accent
- Style Target: Her speech should be readable but unmistakably accented. The accent is part of her class, craft, family pressure, and inherited-dwarf identity. It should carry authority, not parody.
- Example Direction: `Ye're askin' me to trust a scroll that brought soldiers to my door. That's a bold first impression, Moderator.`

### Witness of Horror / Regret

- Status: **Explicit phonetic-spelling exception.**
- Applies to: The later Witness presentation of Horror / Regret, not automatically to José Mateo Navarro in historical council scenes.
- Voice Basis: Mexican cultural cadence plus the Arachne Witness body's wet lisp around long black teeth.
- Default Behavior: Horror's lisp should be represented directly in dialogue with controlled phonetic spelling.
- Core Rule: The lisp is a physical Witness-form effect, not a joke and not a generic Mexican accent marker.
- Acceptable tools:
  - limited `s` / `st` / `sh` pressure where the mouth-form causes distortion
  - recurring substitutions like `story` → `thtory`, `stories` → `thtories`, `scene` → `thcene` when the lisp is meant to be textually audible
  - occasional softened or wet consonant effects around threat, grief, or intimacy
  - Mexican cadence through rhythm and sentence shape, not caricatured spelling
- Avoid:
  - applying the lisp to José's human historical voice by default
  - turning every sibilant into a gimmick
  - using phonetic spelling so dense that Horror becomes unserious
  - treating the lisp as comedy
- Style Target: Horror should sound terrifying, mournful, and intimate. The lisp should make her speech feel physically inhuman and tragic, not silly.
- Example Direction: `Welcome to the thcene between thtories.`

## Continuity Warning

Brynn and Horror are special cases. Their phonetic spelling is intentional because the canon explicitly calls it out. Do not generalize this treatment to other accented characters unless a later canon file explicitly adds them to this exception list.
