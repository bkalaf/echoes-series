## Use Rules

- The First Mirror council members should be called **council members** in Addie-facing Book 01 prose, not Architects.
- “Architect” is an author/canon term and later-series reveal term.
- Accent / cultural influence comes from `WITNESSES.md` plus later explicit corrections and must carry to **both** the human council member / Architect and the later Witness presentation.
- Accent notes are generally performance/voice cues, not phonetic-spelling instructions.
- Current explicit phonetic-default exceptions: **Brynn H. Stonevain** for accent, and **Horror / Regret** for the Witness lisp.
- Ethnicity/culture notes are Earth-origin coding for drafting, visual design, and voice direction.
- Visual descriptions are scaffolded here. Use [Visual Bible — Image Pass Notes](./14_VISUAL_BIBLE_IMAGE_PASS.md) for the current image-derived before/after design logic and refinement notes.
- Witness-form descriptions should support iconography, scene blocking, and image generation, but the human council form remains primary for First Mirror prose.

## Accent Use Rule

The accent/cultural influence source comes from `WITNESSES.md` plus later explicit corrections.

Default series rule:

- Use rhythm, diction, class markers, formality, idiom pressure, and sentence structure.
- Do **not** write most accents with heavy phonetic spelling.

Explicit phonetic-default exceptions:

- **Brynn H. Stonevain** — accent is rendered phonetically by default in direct dialogue.
- **Horror / Regret** — the Witness lisp is rendered phonetically by default in direct Witness dialogue.

Even for exceptions, phonetic rendering should remain readable, intentional, and consistent rather than exaggerated.

## First Mirror Usage Note

In Book 01 First Mirror prose, the reader should hear one recoverable value-line from every council member, but the full later compromise logic remains hidden / pressure-obscured.

The goal is rereadability:

> Later readers should realize every council member already named the exact value they would betray.
