Built from user-provided narration after `01_14_the_law_telling_us_to_forget.md` and the yellow-beacon Witness planning notes.

This chapter begins after the companions leave the White Tower Archives under yellow light and go as one group toward the Luminthalas beacon / obelisk.

Working title chosen from the core fourth-vision reveal:

- **The Cities That Disobeyed**

## Title Rationale

The title points at the political mechanism behind the fourth vision: Dovren punishes Human founding capitals that refused his order, rewards the replacement cities that offer fealty, and turns the triad split into an economic weapon.

It should sound simple at first and horrifying in retrospect.

# Chapter Function

This chapter should convert the yellow beacon from proof into consequence.

The companions have solved the Ariadne / founding-history thread well enough to trigger the yellow checkpoint. Now they must see what the thread leads to:

1. the end of a catastrophe vision where José abandons people to a city-killing wave;
2. a fourth civic-founding vision where Dovren celebrates separation, rewards obedience, and punishes disobedient Human capitals;
3. a return beat where the companions are too shaken to keep pretending Mae's manor is simply Mae's private house.

This chapter should be long, heavy, and emotionally destabilizing.

# Beat-By-Beat Skeleton

## Subchapter 1 — Yellow At Their Backs

1. Begin with the companions leaving the White Tower / Luminthalas archive route under yellow light.
2. The city has changed color around them.
3. The earlier reverent wrist-turn gesture is no longer merely supportive; it now feels like people are watching history happen.
4. Addie should feel the terror of being correct.
5. Gregory is already pale because he understands that the next step is not more research but Witness contact.
6. Brickett tries to fill the fear with jokes.
7. Mae is too quiet.
8. Tallandra watches crowd flow and possible threats but cannot read a threat big enough to explain what they are walking toward.
9. The chapter should move efficiently toward the yellow Witness transition.

## Subchapter 2 — The Last Ships

1. The vision picks up near the end of the prior catastrophe / ship sequence.
2. José stands near the evacuation / pier / port crisis point holding his black command box.
3. He is giving clipped orders through it.
4. Two ships have already cleared or are clearing their moorings.
5. They push for open water.
6. José orders the remaining two ships to pull away.
7. Those ships begin disconnecting and leaving the pier.
8. People remain behind.
9. The vision should show panic and abandonment, not graphic carnage.
10. The companions understand that command decisions are being made in real time and that not everyone is being saved.
11. Addie should notice José's face, not just the ships: he is acting like a man trying to reduce loss, but also like a man making a choice that damns people.

## Subchapter 3 — Get Us In The Air

1. José climbs into the helicopter.
2. The sky is at dusk: dim enough that details blur, bright enough that the approaching shape is visible.
3. José looks toward the water.
4. He sees the wave.
5. Required line: **"Oh Jesus Christ. Get us in the air now."**
6. The companions see it too.
7. The wave is enormous, city-killing in implication.
8. People on the pier begin screaming and running.
9. Carrio or Brickett should produce the blunt holy-shit reaction.
10. The ships go full speed.
11. They appear to drive up the face of the wave or climb through impossible force.
12. The effect should look like magic / impossible propulsion to the companions.
13. None of them should understand the mechanism.
14. From their angle, the ships look as if they are riding the wave up.
15. Hidden truth for later payoff: the ships are actually going airborne to avoid being smashed.
16. Do not say that here.
17. Do not show the wave impact.
18. Cut on the impossible upward motion and the screams behind them.
19. Shift into the fourth vision.

## Subchapter 4 — The Cities That Disobeyed

1. The fourth vision opens on a formal civic ceremony / proclamation space.
2. Dovren stands at the center of the political stage.
3. The tone should be ceremonial on the surface and rotten underneath.
4. Dovren congratulates the founding / elevation of the new cities.
5. He frames the separation of peoples out of the Human cities as vindication.
6. He speaks to the Human cities that remained loyal and to the replacement cities that accepted his structure.
7. His language should be ugly and revealing:
   - **mongrels**;
   - **half-breeds**;
   - Human cities as the proper center;
   - separated populations as proof that blending failed.
8. The companions should not all understand the political machinery at once, but the disgust should be immediate.
9. Mae should recognize the formality of the event before she understands its crime.
10. Matthieu should start tracking who is present and who is absent.

## Subchapter 5 — Unyielding Support

1. Dovren names the cities that refused to send representatives:
   - Khembal;
   - Qinyue;
   - Skjarnheim.
2. He does not sound wounded; he sounds pleased to have examples.
3. He makes it clear that refusal has consequences.
4. He then begrudgingly congratulates the representatives of:
   - Luminthalas;
   - Grendhollow;
   - Nox Vaelor.
5. Replace any earlier overly crude obedience line with formal servility.
6. Required line shape from an obedient representative:

> "You have our unyielding support, High Sovereign. Whatever you require, we stand ready."

7. The line should sound like loyalty and begging at the same time.
8. Dovren accepts the submission as his due.
9. He should make clear that loyalty is rewarded with capital status, trade, protection, and patronage.
10. The companions should realize that the regional capital system is being used as a leash.

## Subchapter 6 — So Much For All-Knowing

1. Dovren refers to Mother's plan without naming her plainly enough for full clarity.
2. Required idea / line:

> "I told her this plan of blending mongrels, half-breeds, and humans would not work. So much for all-knowing."

3. This should clearly point toward Mother for the reader who is tracking the clues.
4. It should still leave enough mystery that the companions cannot fully explain what they are hearing.
5. Addie should feel the word **her** catch in her memory.
6. Gregory should understand that the founding triads were ideological, not accidental.
7. Mae should hear the beginning of institutional racism in the language of statecraft.
8. Yurislav should be shaken because this is Dovren not as rumor or propaganda but as architect of hierarchy.

## Subchapter 7 — The Price Of Refusal

1. Dovren explains, implies, or demonstrates what losing regional capital status means.
2. Trade routes are redirected.
3. Administrative authority moves away.
4. Tax offices, contracts, official banking / currency flows, and supply priorities are transferred to loyal cities.
5. Use the currency name **dovren** if money is referenced.
6. Losing status means losing investment.
7. Losing investment means poverty.
8. Poverty forces people into cheap labor.
9. Cheap labor feeds Dovren's system.
10. This is not a symbolic punishment. It is economic strangulation.
11. Dovren's message is simple: disobey the Sovereign and your city becomes poor.
12. The companions should feel the scale of the trap. This is how cities are made to kneel without armies marching immediately.

## Subchapter 8 — Van'Kareth In The Room

1. Among the Nox Vaelor delegation, include an early Van'Kareth / first Van'Kareth representative.
2. Mae should recognize enough to feel the blow:
   - family name;
   - crest;
   - posture;
   - mannerism;
   - political confidence;
   - the way the representative speaks to power.
3. Do not make Mae responsible for the ancestor's action.
4. Do make her understand that her family benefited from a structure she does not yet understand.
5. The early Van'Kareth figure can offer polished fealty without sounding weak.
6. That should be worse: Nox Vaelor's representative is not simply terrified, but calculating.
7. Mae should go cold rather than visibly explode.
8. Addie notices Mae's stillness.
9. This beat preserves the discomfort around Mae's family being present without collapsing her character into guilt.

## Subchapter 9 — Our Manor

1. After the visions, return the companions to the active / physical present.
2. Everyone is shaken.
3. Mae says they are going back to **our manor**.
4. Someone catches it and says: **"Our manor?"**
5. Mae snaps that not a word about this happens until they get to the house.
6. Then she confirms the pronoun.
7. Required Mae speech:

> "Yes. Our manor. It is abundantly clear to me right now that we have no idea what we are doing, or what our history really is, if what we just saw is real. Yes, our manor. This is so much bigger than us. My misplaced pronoun is probably the least of our fucking worries."

8. This should be a sharp, raw, protective Mae moment.
9. The accidental pronoun signals that she now thinks of the group as a shared household / operational unit.
10. But Mae is right: the pronoun is not the crisis.

## Subchapter 10 — It Was That Goddamn Bad

1. Gregory starts hurling.
2. Everyone looks down at him.
3. Rhün pats his back.
4. Addie pulls out an H&H for him.
5. Gregory's vomiting should be darkly funny but not trivial.
6. It confirms that the vision was morally and intellectually overwhelming.
7. Mae takes the words.
8. Required line:

> "Yes. It was that goddamn bad."

9. End with the group moving toward the manor / estate, carrying the knowledge that they do not understand their own nation.
10. The next chapter can begin at the manor with controlled debrief, or cut to the next layer of the Witness sequence if pacing requires.

# Reader Takeaway

The reader should come out of this chapter understanding:

1. The triad system was not politically innocent.
2. One Human city, one Mythos city, and one Beastfolk city existed in each founding pocket for reasons still not fully explained.
3. Dovren exploited those categories.
4. Khembal, Qinyue, and Skjarnheim disobeyed him and were punished.
5. Luminthalas, Grendhollow, and Nox Vaelor rose because the replacement cities accepted or benefited from Dovren's order.
6. The punishment was economic: redirect trade, starve prestige, force cheap labor.
7. The currency being named **dovren** makes his domination literal in everyday exchange.
8. The pact / forgetting law still has not fully surfaced; save that for later visions.
