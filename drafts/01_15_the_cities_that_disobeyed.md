## Title Rationale

The title points at the political mechanism behind the Dovren vision: Dovren punishes Human founding capitals that refused his order, rewards the replacement cities that offer fealty, and turns the triad split into an economic weapon.

It should sound simple at first and horrifying in retrospect.

This chapter should be long, heavy, and emotionally destabilizing.

8. This should be a sharp, raw, protective Mae moment.
9. The accidental pronoun signals that she now thinks of the group as a shared household / operational unit.

# Reader Takeaway

The reader should come out of this chapter understanding:

1. The triad system was not politically innocent.
2. One Human city, one Mythos city, and one Beastfolk city existed in each founding pocket for reasons still not fully explained.
3. Dovren exploited those categories.
4. Khembal, Qinyue, and Skjarnheim disobeyed him and were punished.
5. Luminthalas, Grendhollow, and Nox Vaelor rose because the replacement cities accepted or benefited from Dovren's order.
6. The punishment was economic: redirect trade, starve prestige, force cheap labor.
7. The currency being named **dovren** makes his domination literal in everyday exchange.
8. The pact / forgetting law still has not fully surfaced; save that for later visions.
