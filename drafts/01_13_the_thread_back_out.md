# Beat-By-Beat Skeleton

## Subchapter 1 — The Uncomfortable Frog

1. The room is crowded by attention, not bodies.
2. Every eye fixes on Barnaby.
3. Barnaby sits awkwardly on an upholstered chair, smoothing nonexistent wrinkles from his vest.
4. He asks why everyone is looking at him.
5. Mae smiles warmly and asks him to pretend they have never heard the story.
6. Barnaby is confused because to him the story is ordinary.
7. Gregory asks permission to guide the telling.
8. Mae grants it immediately.
9. Gregory tells Barnaby not merely to recount events, but to tell them why the story matters to him.
10. Barnaby is thrown by the personal framing but accepts.

## Subchapter 2 — Old Mythology

1. Barnaby says it is only old mythology.
2. A few companions relax, thinking this will be simple folklore.
3. Barnaby says he always liked old myths from the old republics everyone learned about.
4. Matthieu offers the expected Eidolon reference: Thalorion.
5. Barnaby corrects him matter-of-factly: **Ancient Greece**.
6. The room goes quiet.
7. Barnaby gestures vaguely toward “before all of this.”
8. This should land as wrong / ancient / unplaced, but do not stop the scene for a lore lecture.
9. Barnaby describes humanity as primitive in the old story-world: sticks, stones, barely fire.
10. Addie leans forward at the repeated ancientness of it.

## Subchapter 3 — The Maze That Moved

1. Barnaby explains he liked the stories because they had a point, a lesson.
2. He jokes that this one had thread, and he is a tailor.
3. He struggles with names: Theseus becomes Ethius / Etheus; Minos becomes Midas / Minos.
4. Gregory may twitch at the imprecision but should not interrupt.
5. Barnaby emphasizes that the names are less important than the shape of the story.
6. There is a princess, but Barnaby corrects the romance: she loved him; he did not necessarily love her.
7. Her father sends young people into a giant maze.
8. Inside is a Minotaur: half man, half bull.
9. It kills everyone who enters.
10. Barnaby says the monster is not the most dangerous part.
11. The dangerous part is that the maze moves.
12. Passages change behind the person who enters.
13. Knowing the way in does not mean knowing the way out.
14. The room becomes still because “maze that moves” echoes the current Conjunction logic.
15. Mae’s coin may stop.
16. Across the room, Max looks at Mae.
17. Neither of them says anything.
18. Addie notices the look and files it in her back room, but has no context for it yet.
19. Do not explain the Bellwether house magic here.

## Subchapter 4 — The Thread Back Out

1. Barnaby says the princess gives the hero two gifts.
2. One is a sword.
3. One is a red ball / spool of thread.
4. Barnaby brightens because this is his favorite part.
5. The hero ties one end of the thread to the entrance before going in.
6. He carries the rest into the maze.
7. He kills the Minotaur.
8. Barnaby pauses and realizes aloud that the sword alone would have been enough to kill the monster.
9. But without the thread, the hero would die trapped after winning.
10. Barnaby states the core lesson: what is the point of defeating something if you cannot find your way back afterward?
11. Let silence settle. This is the chapter’s thematic center.

## Subchapter 5 — Finest Tailors in the Realm

1. Addie asks if Barnaby’s people all know this story.
2. Barnaby brightens and says most do.
3. He names his people as the finest tailors in the realm.
4. He shows the small crimson spool on his belt.
5. The story is close to their hearts; parents tell it to children; every young tailor eventually hears it.
6. Mae asks if anyone has questions.
7. No one asks, not because they have none, but because none feel worthy yet.
8. Mae thanks Barnaby and dismisses him.
9. Addie stops him before he leaves.
10. She thanks him for the traveling clothes, naming their comfort and craft.
11. Barnaby is deeply touched, cheeks turning green.
12. He says he would do anything for Lady Mae, then quietly tells Addie she is becoming a close second.
13. He thanks Addie for asking about a story that mattered to him.
14. This beat matters: Addie’s respect for craft earns Barnaby’s loyalty without command.
15. Barnaby immediately pivots to Brynn’s clothes, muttering measurements and material choices as he leaves.
16. His exit should be comic, observant, and craft-driven.

## Subchapter 6 — Almost Correct

1. The room remains silent until Barnaby’s voice fades down the hall.
2. Carrio asks whether anyone else thinks that is probably the answer to the riddle.
3. Several people nod.
4. Gregory says maybe.
5. Addie teases him because of course he would say maybe.
6. Gregory smiles but insists they should be certain.
7. He warns that Witnesses may hand them something almost correct.
8. Almost-correct answers are dangerous because mistakes happen there.
9. This keeps tension alive and prevents Barnaby’s story from feeling too easy.

## Subchapter 7 — One Tiny Comma

1. Brickett admits he still does not understand the riddle.
2. Carrio agrees.
3. Yurislav agrees.
4. Matthieu says they have been staring at it for hours and it still reads strangely.
5. Brynn asks what the riddle is.
6. Mae gives her the parchment.
7. Brynn reads it once and immediately says they are reading it wrong.
8. The room stares.
9. Brynn explains that people with her accent get misunderstood constantly.
10. She asks if Horror read it slowly, one word at a time.
11. Addie says yes.
12. Brynn says that is exactly how Horror wanted Addie to hear it.
13. Brynn lays the parchment flat and makes one tiny mark: a comma.
14. She turns it around and tells them to read it again.
15. No word changes.
16. Only the pause changes.
17. The riddle now reads: **That makes the horned, dead charge.**
18. Gregory sees it first or nearly first.
19. Mae smiles.
20. Carrio swears softly.
21. Brickett has a delayed “Oh.”
22. Addie registers the size of the mark against the size of the shift.

## Subchapter 8 — Where to Begin

1. The group understands that “horned dead” was the wrong grouping.
2. The phrase is **horned, dead charge**.
3. The comma points toward the dead charging, and toward a horned element as qualifier / separate clue.
4. Do not solve the full mission route yet unless the next chapter requires it.
5. Mae says it looks like they finally know where to begin.
6. End with the room oriented toward action rather than explanation.
