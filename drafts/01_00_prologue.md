# 01_00_prologue — The Mother Who Started the War

## Detail Level
L4 Reconstruction Outline

## Purpose
Establish the series frame: the story is simultaneously true and false, science and magic are linked, Mother is running out of time, and the first act of war is an act of compassion toward Brickett.

## POV / Narrative Mode
Reader-facing omniscient narrator, addressing Dear Reader directly, then shifting into mythic omniscient focus on Mother in Skyrend Peak.

## Timeline Position
Two days before the summer solstice, before the first modern Conjunction initialization. Mother has sixteen months left before her protection ends.

## Cast Present
- Narrator / Dear Reader voice.
- Mother / Dianne.
- Brickett H. asleep — a homeless 14-year-old beaver beastfolk.
- Background astronomers, mentioned but not active.

## Beat-by-Beat Skeleton
1. Narrator opens with the need to tell a story.
2. The story is framed as entirely true and entirely false depending on source and perspective.
3. Narrator lists competing possible beginnings: end, city gates, or the earlier meeting of eighteen people.
4. Narrator admits timeline confusion and warns the reader.
5. Narrator promises the first pages contain the solution to the whole series.
6. Narrator apologizes for science and mathematics.
7. Science and magic are framed as competitive cousins.
8. Narrator accidentally addresses Addie, then corrects themself.
9. Mother is introduced as ancient, tired, grandmotherly, and nearly out of time.
10. The fantasy beach opening is rejected; the story begins at Skyrend Peak.
11. Skyrend Peak’s freezing slums are established two days before summer solstice.
12. Astronomers panic in the background over the three moons.
13. Mother searches for one forgotten child.
14. She finds Brickett asleep in garbage inside a rotting warehouse.
15. Brickett should read as unmistakably beaver: damp brown fur, broad flat tail tucked badly under him, big front teeth, muddy paws, and singed patches from failed fire magic.
16. Mother revives the dying fire.
17. The revived flame matters to Brickett visually: even asleep, his world is marked by damp wood, cold mud, and a dangerous attraction to fire.
18. She leaves the backpack and sealed scroll.
19. She notices Brickett shivering.
20. The blanket is identified as not part of the plan.
21. Mother creates the wool blanket out of compassion.
22. Mother whispers faith in Brickett, Addie, and the others.
23. Mother tells him he must hold the line when the clock runs out.
24. Mother says she loves him; Brickett does not hear it, but the reader does.
25. Narrator introduces the repeating number pattern: 3, 9, 18, 24, 72.
26. The wax seal is tied to that sequence.
27. Narrator refuses to explain the pattern yet.
28. Narrator reveals Mother has started a war with backpack, scroll, blanket, and loophole.
29. Final image: Brickett asleep as the scroll waits for morning.

## Continuity Notes
- Check older phrasing around “24 sovereign territories” and “72 distinct cultures” against current one-nation / city-state canon before prose lock.
- Brickett is a 14-year-old beaver beastfolk, not a raccoon.
- Brickett’s fire obsession / fire-mage desire can be seeded through soot, singed fur, failed fire scraps, and the dying campfire, but he should still be asleep in this prologue.
- Mother’s spontaneous blanket is a key moral seed: compassion modifies duty.

## Final Image
A sleeping, soot-singed beaver child, a backpack, a dying fire revived by Mother, and Mother running out of time.
