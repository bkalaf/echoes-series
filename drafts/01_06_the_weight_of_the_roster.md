# Subchapter 1 — The Bronze Doors

## Subchapter Function
Bring Addie from public exhaustion into institutional awe and procedural danger. This subchapter establishes the Conclave chamber, the roster wall, Addie’s role, and Brickett’s attaché designation.

## Beat-by-Beat Skeleton
1. Addie approaches the monumental bronze doors of the Conclave High Assembly.
2. The doors are eighty feet high in current political reality, though Addie remembers reading they were once only fifteen.
3. Addie recognizes power physically expanding architecture to match its self-image.
4. Addie is too exhausted and nauseated to care about architecture except as a floor that hopefully will not shatter beneath her.
5. Boss and Barry leave her at the threshold because procedural texts say the ancient enchantments are not worth testing.
6. Boss offers practical encouragement: do not throw up.
7. Barry offers sincere but useless good luck.
8. Addie notes Boss is taking Brickett to the Concord barracks to be bathed, and she envies that task as a coping mechanism.
9. Addie adjusts the blue robe with white trim, unaware or unwilling to process that it is sitting wrong.
10. The doors open with a pressurized vault-like hiss.
11. Addie enters alone.
12. The High Assembly reveals itself as a massive half-moon atrium beneath a synthetic blue sky.
13. Addie’s back room maps the chamber instantly: exits, seats, banners, light angles, representative faces.
14. The room contains the seventy-two representatives, divided into twenty-four regional sections, each subdivided into thirds.
15. One representative has eight eyes; Addie chooses not to process that today.
16. Around the inner wall are black-iron framed vellum notices.
17. Addie recognizes the same scroll-notice format from outside.
18. The notice explains the Moderator role: Facilitator amended to Moderator, inheriting functions of the temporarily defunct role plus additional duties.
19. The notice says the list will grow as people are informed and accept their roles.
20. Addie sees eleven clean slots: nine numbered, two alpha-enumerated.
21. Slot one is already filled with Addie as Head Moderator.
22. Alpha slot A shows Brickett H as personal attaché to the Head Moderator.
23. Addie realizes Brickett misread “attaché” as “attached” and decided he had been physically assigned to her like luggage with opinions and fire risk.
24. Addie suppresses inappropriate laughter and keeps walking.

# Subchapter 3 — Four Scrolls

## Subchapter Function
Move from Conclave authority to roster authority. Four scrolls appear and each recipient’s opening clarifies a different aspect of the system: Mae’s involuntary institutional role, Carrio’s analytic precision, Rhün’s alpha designation, and Yurislav’s personal-name sovereignty against the High Sovereign’s office.

2. Addie immediately counts four, not five, because her own name is already confirmed by crossing the threshold under blue beacon.
3. The first scroll moves toward Mae.
4. The second moves toward Carrio H. Vessalor, a black cobra serpent-kin in the lower Schism bloc.
5. Carrio wears Mark of the Orbs earrings; Addie notices him noticing that she noticed.
6. The third scroll moves toward Rhün H. Ignis in the Crucible section, a magma elemental whose existence had been classified as illegal until six years ago.
7. The fourth scroll moves toward Yurislav H. Arslan behind the High Sovereign.
8. The Overseer tells Yurislav he will want to hold onto that scroll.
9. Dovren silently claws at his chair.
10. Mae opens first only after stating, on the record, that she is opening under protest and only to read.
11. The Overseer says the record is aware.
12. Mae asks who keeps the record.
13. The Overseer answers: something with better memory than her.
14. Mae breaks the seal.
15. Green flame erupts and the wall fills slot seven: `7 — LADY MAE’VYRI H. VAN’KARETH`.
16. The chamber erupts into contained professional chaos.
17. Mae insists she did not accept anything.
18. The Overseer explains that opening a sealed appointment under active Conjunction authority constitutes acknowledgment of receipt, eligibility, and provisional acceptance pending survival.
19. Mae and Addie exchange a look: neither knew, both are furious, and they will revisit this.
20. Mae asks what seventh means.
21. The Overseer says the list will explain itself as it completes.
22. Mae demands the full answer.
23. The Overseer says she wants the last page while still on the first chapter.
24. Mae sets the scroll down instead of throwing it and says the conversation is not finished.
25. Carrio opens next with slow professional indifference.
26. Slot five fills: `5 — CARRIO H. VESSALOR`.
27. Carrio asks whether the numbering is positional or sequential.
28. The Overseer asks him to define the distinction.
29. Carrio explains positional as rank/authority and sequential as order of selection.
30. The Overseer answers: neither.
31. Carrio asks what it is.
32. The Overseer says it is the order in which the story requires him.
33. Carrio treats that as valuable information and jokes that the apocalypse remembered to accessorize.
34. A nearby fox-kin shifts away from Carrio; he notices and does not comment.
35. Rhün opens his scroll without waiting.
36. Green flame interacts oddly with Rhün’s own molten authority.
37. The wall writes not to a numbered slot but to alpha line B: `B — RHÜN H. IGNIS`.
38. Addie realizes alpha designations mean a categorically different function from numbered roles.
39. Addie compares Brickett’s alpha attaché role with Rhün’s untitled alpha role.
40. Rhün reads his own first name in a chamber that usually reduces him to surname/policy shorthand.
41. Rhün gives a private, loaded “hm.”
42. Mae demands to know what the lettering indicates.
43. The Overseer says a different function.
44. Mae asks him to define different.
45. He says different from numbered.
46. Mae realizes he is doing this deliberately.
47. The Overseer says he is answering accurately and recommends she develop tolerance for partial answers.
48. Yurislav has not moved.
49. The Overseer tells him to open it.
50. Yurislav calmly assesses the implications of complying.
51. Yurislav explains his conflict: he is posted as High Sovereign’s personal guard, but the scroll appeared in his hands.
52. He wants to know whether it is addressed to the Sovereign’s office or to him personally.
53. The chamber recognizes the precision of the objection.
54. Mae considers Yurislav with new attention.
55. The Overseer says the scroll appeared in Yurislav’s hands because it is addressed to Yurislav H. Arslan.
56. The Sovereign’s office has no claim on Yurislav’s name.
57. Dovren makes a non-word sound of fury.
58. Yurislav opens the scroll.
59. Slot eight fills: `8 — YURISLAV H. ARSLAN`.
60. The room understands that this is not just political; a young guard has been chosen out from under the king he serves.
