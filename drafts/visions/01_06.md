# 01_06 — The Starter Cities / The Well of Souls

## Status

L4 vision reconstruction draft.

## Placement

Book 01 Vision 6.

Insert after `drafts/visions/01_05.md` inside `drafts/01_19_the_well_of_souls.md`.

This vision follows the José / Noel Nikolai headquarters vision and precedes the companions’ return from the Scene Between Stories.

## Core Scene

Young Dovren / DJT and an early, more emotionally expressive Overseer proxy speak alone in a landlocked throne-city chamber after Mother’s first major population-balance failure.

The scene reveals early collaboration between Dovren and Mother/Overseer without fully explaining the machinery. It should make the logistics of early Eidolon more suspicious, not less.

## Setting

Historical / mirror layer.

A throne room in an early landlocked construction city. Addie initially assumes she is seeing Aquila Matara again, but then realizes there is no coastline visible from the windows.

- This is not clearly modern Aquila Matara.
- It is not the later throne scene with Geoffrik and Dovren's spouses.
- No Geoffrik appears.
- No spouses appear.
- No guards actively intervene.
- The room is under construction or early in its institutional life.
- The city feels wrong enough that Addie has to reframe her assumptions about prior visions.

## Vision Function

This vision should reveal / seed:

1. Mother miscalculated human narrative continuity by treating wiped humans as if they could simply be turned on mid-story like AI processes.
2. The first twenty-four cities erupted into civil unrest because humans, beastfolk, and mythos were split into neat thirds and sorted themselves into visible commonalities.
3. Mother treated outward and hidden traits as mathematical balances, but human identity did not cancel out to zero.
4. The Well of Souls / soul-storage system was under pressure because too many people were dying or waiting for embodiment.
5. The later forty-eight cities were seeded deliberately as expansion / relief-valve starter cities, not purely organic settlements.
6. The starter cities required beacon-network compatibility, seed-of-power structure, hidden infrastructure, and staged construction cover.
7. Dovren understood the social/political exploitation opportunity faster than Mother did.
8. Mother recognized fallibility and the need for fallback / worst-case planning.
9. Dovren dismissed the quantum possibility-check system because it was not a simple prediction engine.
10. The historical Overseer begins emotionally shutting down once arguing with Dovren proves useless.

This vision should plant questions:

- How did power shift outside Conjunctions?
- Who actually built the starter cities?
- What is the Well?
- How could populations be moved or born into new cities so fast?
- Why does the Overseer say that if he/it died, everyone would have died?
- How much did Dovren exploit from the beginning?

Do not answer every planted question in Book 1.

# Beat-By-Beat Draft

## Beat 1 — Not Aquila Matara

1. The vision opens in a throne room that feels familiar at first.
2. Addie expects Aquila Matara because of earlier Dovren imagery.
3. She looks through the windows and realizes there is no coastline.
4. This is landlocked.
5. The city is in early construction.
6. Addie realizes two thousand years may have distorted her assumptions.
7. The room contains only young Dovren and the Overseer.
8. No Geoffrik.
9. No spouses.
10. No visible guards.
11. The Overseer feels different: still the Overseer, but with more emotion present.

## Beat 2 — The Deal And The Unrest

1. Dovren rises from the throne and walks toward the windows.
2. He says the Overseer has surprised him by honoring their deal.
3. He says they need a plan because he did not agree to the exact execution she enacted.
4. He says she did not tell him her whole plan.
5. Civil unrest is becoming dangerous.
6. Too much has changed too fast.
7. Dovren says they do not have a chance unless they come to an understanding.
8. The Overseer admits:

> "I fear you are right. I miscalculated the balancing of populations."

9. Dovren stops looking out the window and looks at the Overseer.
10. Required Dovren answer:

> "You think?"

## Beat 3 — The Twenty-Four Cities

1. Dovren says nearly all twenty-four cities are having struggles for power.
2. The populations divided themselves into the singular commonalities they could find.
3. Because the cities were divided into neat thirds, Mother made sorting easy.
4. Dovren says he could have helped.
5. He could have told her the plan was bad.
6. The Overseer says he is probably right.
7. Then the Overseer adds:

> "But truth is, I don't trust you, DJT."

8. Dovren says:

> "And you probably shouldn't. But we need to fix this."

## Beat 4 — Land Grabs And Exile

1. Dovren explains the other crisis: no defined borders.
2. City-states have started an all-out land grab.
3. Every city except one is participating.
4. The exception is not virtuous; it is expelling one group and is too internally consumed to grab land.
5. That city will be ripe for conquest later.
6. Dovren looks directly at the Overseer and says her well must be full.
7. The Overseer admits she will struggle to empty it.
8. Do not over-explain the Well here; let the companions hear enough to know there is a dangerous population/soul-storage pressure system.

## Beat 5 — Beasts, Mythos, And Weaponized Language

1. Dovren says more cities need to be built.
2. Expansion must be allowed.
3. He says they need to let the beasts and the other group separate out.
4. He asks what she is calling the other group.
5. The Overseer says:

> "Mythos."

6. Dovren calls them half-breeds.
7. The Overseer says she does not like that and says they are not half-breeds.
8. Dovren says that is what he will call them because it is easiest to weaponize.
9. Required Dovren idea:

> "You do not understand people or politics enough to know how you weaponize against and how you weaponize for. The whole point is to split them."

10. Carrio should understand this line sharply later.

## Beat 6 — Starter Cities

1. Dovren says new cities will give populations a fresh start.
2. They will create incentives for population growth.
3. They will reduce pressure in the Well.
4. The Overseer agrees it is a good plan but says cities are not arbitrary.
5. The original cities were constructed at specific points to focus Conjunctions and the beacon network.
6. They cannot just found cities anywhere.
7. The cities need certain structures:
   - seed of power;
   - beacon linkage;
   - hidden network structure;
   - correct geography and climate placement;
   - enough starter infrastructure to receive people.
8. Dovren tells her to determine where they should be.
9. He asks how much construction work she can do without making the truth obvious.
10. The Overseer realizes what he is proposing: starter cities.
11. Dovren compares it to ordering something back there and getting it delivered in two days.
12. This should sound glib and obscene given what he is describing.

## Beat 7 — The Forty-Eight

1. After a silence, the Overseer says she has a good position that meets the climate necessary for the forty-eight.
2. Dovren says he does not care about the details.
3. He wants to know how they are splitting them up.
4. He will call a meeting of the twenty-four current leaders.
5. He will encourage new people to leave to cities being built, ostensibly to start construction.
6. The Overseer offers to boost logistics and production supplies.
7. She wants a hardier working population supported during the shift.
8. Hidden author-facing logic:
   - the Concord guard / construction crews provide cover;
   - Mother provides hidden seed structures;
   - people believe they are founding cities more naturally than they are.
9. Do not make the mechanics too explicit in-character yet.

## Beat 8 — Buying Time

1. The Overseer says quelling the fighting will be harder.
2. Dovren suggests doing what he did in the old times: bribe people not to fight.
3. It will buy time.
4. It will thin cities.
5. It will separate groups.
6. It will let factions develop.
7. Dovren is already thinking exploitatively.
8. Mother is still thinking stabilizing/corrective.
9. The same plan serves two different motives.

## Beat 9 — Quantum Side

1. Dovren asks whether she has checked the quantum side of things.
2. The Overseer says she has checked as much as she feels comfortable checking.
3. She explains that it is not predictive.
4. It is a possibility check.
5. It is an indicator of whether they are moving in a promising direction.
6. It does not tell them how to correct course.
7. Dovren says he would rather have something that says yes or no.
8. Required Overseer idea:

> "You want a magic eight ball. It is not that simple."

9. Dovren dismisses it.
10. The Overseer says they have one shot, and if they fail, they do not get to restore the game.
11. Dovren interrupts:

> "I already told you I don't care."

12. The Overseer begins emotionally checking out.
13. This is the pivot toward the later affectless Overseer behavior.

## Beat 10 — Start Over

1. The Overseer says that because the world is still new, worst case, they could start over.
2. Dovren asks whether she would actually do that.
3. He asks how many times they would start over until it was perfect or merely okay.
4. He says it would be a never-ending cycle.
5. They would never get anything off the ground.
6. He says they have to go forward.
7. This is one of the rare moments where his practical objection has weight.
8. He is also happy because he sees how to exploit the mess.
9. He sits back on the throne with an ugly smile.
10. He says he never thought he could work with her after what she did.

## Beat 11 — He Would Have Killed You

1. The Overseer says coldly:

> "I did what was necessary. I've told you the story. He would have killed us all."

2. Dovren answers:

> "No. He would have killed you."

3. The Overseer turns toward where the companions stand.
4. The eye contact should be direct and creepy.
5. The Overseer raises a paw and waves.
6. Looking over the shoulder to Dovren, the Overseer says:

> "DJT, that is the exact same thing. If he had killed me, we would have all died. In fact, we still might."

7. The Overseer steps into shadow.
8. As the vision fades, Dovren says:

> "What the hell does that fucking bitch mean by that?"

# Author-Facing Hidden Mechanics

These should guide the writing but not all be explained directly:

- Mother put humans, beastfolk, and mythos together in the first twenty-four cities in mathematical thirds.
- Beastfolk represent outward-expression traits / visible behavioral patterns.
- Mythos represent inward / hidden traits.
- Mother assumed visible and hidden traits would balance like positive and negative values.
- Humans do not work that way because people contain hidden traits they do not know about and histories they need to understand themselves.
- Wiped adults and children cannot begin mid-story without psychic/social consequences.
- AI can begin at activation and proceed; humans require origin continuity.
- This becomes one of Mother's fundamental miscalculations.
- The Well of Souls must stay within capacity: too full causes storage/bleed/rebirth impossibility pressure; too empty also causes population logic problems.
- The forty-eight new cities are seeded to relieve pressure and reduce civil unrest.
- Dovren plans to exploit the expansion to create factions and collaborators.
- Mother is trying to stabilize the world, but she is also now learning that her assumptions can be wrong.
