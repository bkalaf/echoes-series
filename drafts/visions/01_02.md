## Core Scene

The vision picks up near the end of a catastrophe / evacuation sequence.

José Mateo Navarro stands at a port / pier / evacuation crisis point holding a black command box and giving orders while ships pull away and civilians are left behind.

## Vision Function

This vision should show panic, abandonment, and command responsibility without showing graphic carnage.

It should make José morally complicated:

- he is acting like a man trying to reduce loss;
- he is also making a choice that damns people;
- the companions must see that both things can be true.

This vision leads directly into `01_03 — Get Us In The Air`.

# Setting / Visual Language

- A waterfront / pier / port crisis point.
- Evacuation infrastructure under stress.
- Ships moored close enough that civilians can see them leaving.
- Dusk or near-dusk light beginning to flatten details.
- People gathered at the pier / waterfront, frightened and increasingly aware they are not being allowed on.
- José with a black command box, isolated by authority even while standing among crisis.

# Beat-By-Beat Draft

## Beat 1 — The Black Command Box

1. The vision opens with José at the evacuation / pier / port crisis point.
2. He is holding his black command box.
3. He is issuing clipped orders through it.
4. He is not debating anymore. He is executing a plan.
5. The companions should understand immediately that this is command under pressure.

## Beat 2 — First Two Ships Clear

1. Two ships have already cleared or are clearing their moorings.
2. They begin making for open water.
3. Their movement should be massive, ugly, and final.
4. People on the pier understand what it means before the companions do.
5. The ships are not simply sailing. They are leaving.

## Beat 3 — José Orders The Others Out

1. José orders the remaining two ships to pull away.
2. The other two ships begin disconnecting.
3. Mooring lines come loose.
4. Gangways retract or are hauled away.
5. Workers / guards / crew move with practiced panic.
6. The order creates a visible break between those already aboard and those left behind.

## Beat 4 — People Left Behind

1. People remain on the pier / waterfront.
2. They are not abstract casualties.
3. The vision should show fear, pleading, realization, and crowd motion.
4. Do **not** show graphic carnage.
5. Do **not** show the wave impact here.
6. The horror is the moment before the disaster, when abandonment becomes obvious.

## Beat 5 — The Companions Read José

1. The companions understand that decisions are being made in real time.
2. They understand that not everyone is being saved.
3. Addie should notice José's face, not just the ships.
4. José looks like a man trying to reduce loss.
5. José also looks like a man making a choice that damns people.
6. The vision should refuse to make the moral read clean.

## Beat 6 — Transition Into Vision 03

1. José turns toward the helicopter / flying machine.
2. The ships are already pulling away.
3. The civilians remain behind.
4. The air feels wrong before anyone names why.
5. Cut or slide directly into `01_03 — Get Us In The Air`.
