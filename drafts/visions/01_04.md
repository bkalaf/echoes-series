## Core Scene

Dovren I / the First Dovren presides over a formal civic ceremony where he celebrates the founding / elevation of new cities, openly frames the separation of peoples as vindication, rewards obedient replacement capitals, and punishes Human founding capitals that refused to send representatives.

## Vision Function

This vision reveals that the modern capital structure is not politically innocent.

It should show that:

- the original twenty-four city-state pockets were Human-led triads;
- separation into Human / Mythos / Beastfolk civic identities was quickly weaponized;
- Dovren fueled and formalized racism as policy;
- loyalty to Dovren could elevate a replacement capital;
- refusal could economically strangle a city;
- Khembal, Qinyue, and Skjarnheim disobeyed and were punished;
- Luminthalas, Grendhollow, and Nox Vaelor benefited from submission / replacement;
- Mae's family has an early connection to the Nox Vaelor benefit structure.

The full pact / forgetting-law reveal should remain reserved for later visions.

Use lower-case **dovren** when referring to the currency unless it begins a sentence.

# Beat-By-Beat Draft

## Beat 1 — Civic Ceremony Opens

1. The fourth vision opens on a formal civic ceremony / proclamation space.
2. Dovren stands at the center of the political stage.
3. The tone is ceremonial on the surface and rotten underneath.
4. Dovren congratulates the founding / elevation of the new cities.
5. He frames the separation of peoples out of the Human cities as vindication.
6. He speaks to the Human cities that remained loyal and to the replacement cities that accepted his structure.

## Beat 2 — Ugly Language / Separation As Vindication

1. Dovren's language should be ugly and revealing.
2. Required language concepts:
   - **mongrels**;
   - **half-breeds**;
   - Human cities as the proper center;
   - separated populations as proof that blending failed.
3. The companions should not all understand the political machinery at once.
4. The disgust should be immediate.
5. Mae recognizes the formality of the event before she understands its crime.
6. Matthieu starts tracking who is present and who is absent.

## Beat 3 — Cities That Refused

1. Dovren names the Human cities that refused to send representatives:
   - **Khembal**;
   - **Qinyue**;
   - **Skjarnheim**.
2. He does not sound wounded.
3. He sounds pleased to have examples.
4. He makes clear that refusal has consequences.

## Beat 4 — Replacement Capitals Rewarded

1. Dovren begrudgingly congratulates the representatives of:
   - **Luminthalas**;
   - **Grendhollow**;
   - **Nox Vaelor**.
2. Replace any earlier overly crude obedience line with formal servility.
3. Required line shape from an obedient representative:

> "You have our unyielding support, High Sovereign. Whatever you require, we stand ready."

4. The line should sound like loyalty and begging at the same time.
5. Dovren accepts the submission as his due.
6. Dovren makes clear that loyalty is rewarded with capital status, trade, protection, and patronage.
7. The companions realize that the regional capital system is being used as a leash.

## Beat 5 — So Much For All-Knowing

1. Dovren refers to Mother's plan without naming her plainly enough for full clarity.
2. Required idea / line:

> "I told her this plan of blending mongrels, half-breeds, and humans would not work. So much for all-knowing."

3. This should clearly point toward Mother for readers tracking the clues.
4. It should still leave enough mystery that the companions cannot fully explain what they are hearing.
5. Addie should feel the word **her** catch in her memory.
6. Gregory should understand that the founding triads were ideological, not accidental.
7. Mae should hear the beginning of institutional racism in the language of statecraft.
8. Yurislav should be shaken because this is Dovren not as rumor or propaganda but as architect of hierarchy.

## Beat 6 — The Price Of Refusal

1. Dovren explains, implies, or demonstrates what losing regional capital status means.
2. Trade routes are redirected.
3. Administrative authority moves away.
4. Tax offices, contracts, official banking / currency flows, and supply priorities transfer to loyal cities.
5. Use the currency name **dovren** if money is referenced.
6. Losing status means losing investment.
7. Losing investment means poverty.
8. Poverty forces people into cheap labor.
9. Cheap labor feeds Dovren's system.
10. This is not a symbolic punishment.
11. It is economic strangulation.
12. Dovren's message is simple: disobey the Sovereign and your city becomes poor.
13. The companions should feel the scale of the trap.
14. This is how cities are made to kneel without armies marching immediately.

## Beat 7 — Van'Kareth In The Room

1. Among the Nox Vaelor delegation, include an early Van'Kareth / first Van'Kareth representative.
2. Mae should recognize enough to feel the blow:
   - family name;
   - crest;
   - posture;
   - mannerism;
   - political confidence;
   - the way the representative speaks to power.
3. Do not make Mae responsible for the ancestor's action.
4. Do make her understand that her family benefited from a structure she does not yet understand.
5. The early Van'Kareth figure can offer polished fealty without sounding weak.
6. That should be worse: Nox Vaelor's representative is not simply terrified, but calculating.
7. Mae should go cold rather than visibly explode.
8. Addie notices Mae's stillness.
9. This beat preserves the discomfort around Mae's family being present without collapsing her character into guilt.

## Beat 8 — Transition Out

1. Let the companions carry the vision's political horror back into the physical present.
2. The next active-scene beat is the **Our Manor** return beat in `01_15_the_cities_that_disobeyed.md`.
3. Gregory's later vomiting should feel earned by this vision combined with the wave vision.

# Reader Takeaway

The reader should come out of this vision understanding:

1. The triad system was not politically innocent.
2. One Human city, one Mythos city, and one Beastfolk city existed in each founding pocket for reasons still not fully explained.
3. Dovren exploited those categories.
4. Khembal, Qinyue, and Skjarnheim disobeyed him and were punished.
5. Luminthalas, Grendhollow, and Nox Vaelor rose because replacement cities accepted or benefited from Dovren's order.
6. The punishment was economic: redirect trade, starve prestige, force cheap labor.
7. The currency being named **dovren** makes domination literal in everyday exchange.
8. The pact / forgetting law still has not fully surfaced; save that for later visions.
