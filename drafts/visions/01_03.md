## Core Scene

José Mateo Navarro stands at a port / pier / evacuation crisis point holding a black command box and giving orders while ships pull away, civilians are left behind, and the city-killing wave approaches.

He then climbs into the helicopter / flying machine, sees the wave, and orders immediate takeoff.

The ships appear to drive up the face of the wave or climb through impossible force. The companions cannot understand what they are seeing yet.

## Vision Function

This vision should show panic, abandonment, command responsibility, and the scale of the catastrophe without showing graphic carnage or the actual wave impact.

It should make José morally complicated:

- he is acting like a man trying to reduce loss;
- he is also making a choice that damns people;
- the companions must see that both things can be true.

It should also plant the later propulsion-system reveal:

- from the companions' angle, the ships look like they are riding up the wave;
- the deeper truth is that they are actually going airborne to avoid being smashed;
- do **not** explain that here.

This vision cuts before impact and shifts into `01_04 — The Cities That Disobeyed`.

# Setting / Visual Language

- A waterfront / pier / port crisis point.
- Evacuation infrastructure under stress.
- Ships moored close enough that civilians can see them leaving.
- Dusk or near-dusk light beginning to flatten details.
- People gathered at the pier / waterfront, frightened and increasingly aware they are not being allowed on.
- José with a black command box, isolated by authority even while standing among crisis.
- Helicopter / flying machine waiting close enough for José to reach it.
- A wave enormous enough to make the companions understand that this is city-killing scale.

# Beat-By-Beat Draft

## Beat 1 — The Black Command Box

1. The vision opens with José at the evacuation / pier / port crisis point.
2. He is holding his black command box.
3. He is issuing clipped orders through it.
4. He is not debating anymore. He is executing a plan.
5. The companions should understand immediately that this is command under pressure.

## Beat 2 — First Two Ships Clear

1. Two ships have already cleared or are clearing their moorings.
2. They begin making for open water.
3. Their movement should be massive, ugly, and final.
4. People on the pier understand what it means before the companions do.
5. The ships are not simply sailing. They are leaving.

## Beat 3 — José Orders The Remaining Ships Out

1. José orders the remaining two ships to pull away.
2. The other two ships begin disconnecting.
3. Mooring lines come loose.
4. Gangways retract or are hauled away.
5. Workers / guards / crew move with practiced panic.
6. The order creates a visible break between those already aboard and those left behind.

## Beat 4 — People Left Behind

1. People remain on the pier / waterfront.
2. They are not abstract casualties.
3. The vision should show fear, pleading, realization, and crowd motion.
4. Do **not** show graphic carnage.
5. Do **not** show the wave impact here.
6. The horror is the moment before the disaster, when abandonment becomes obvious.

## Beat 5 — The Companions Read José

1. The companions understand that decisions are being made in real time.
2. They understand that not everyone is being saved.
3. Addie should notice José's face, not just the ships.
4. José looks like a man trying to reduce loss.
5. José also looks like a man making a choice that damns people.
6. The vision should refuse to make the moral read clean.

## Beat 6 — José Enters The Helicopter

1. José turns toward the helicopter / flying machine.
2. The ships are already pulling away.
3. The civilians remain behind.
4. The air feels wrong before anyone names why.
5. José climbs into the helicopter / flying machine.
6. He is still carrying the momentum of his last command decisions.
7. The scene should feel like there is no time left for reconsideration.

## Beat 7 — He Sees The Wave

1. José looks toward the water.
2. He sees the wave.
3. Required line:

> "Oh Jesus Christ. Get us in the air now."

4. This should be the first moment when José's control breaks into open fear.
5. The companions see the wave too.
6. The wave is enormous, city-killing in implication.

## Beat 8 — The Pier Breaks Into Panic

1. People on the pier begin screaming.
2. They run.
3. They do not have anywhere meaningful to go.
4. Carrio or Brickett can produce the blunt holy-shit reaction.
5. The companions understand that the earlier ship choice has now become immediate death for the people left behind.
6. Do **not** show impact.

## Beat 9 — The Ships Climb

1. The ships go full speed.
2. They appear to drive up the face of the wave or climb through impossible force.
3. The visual should imply some kind of magic / impossible propulsion effect.
4. None of the companions understand the mechanism.
5. From their angle and the dusk conditions, the ships look as though they are riding the wave up.
6. The motion should look impossible.
7. The companions should not yet know whether they are watching escape, impact, or a miracle.

## Beat 10 — Hidden Truth For Later

1. The deeper truth is that the ships are actually going airborne to avoid being smashed.
2. Do **not** explicitly say this in the vision.
3. A later vision / Mother conversation can reveal that the emergency forced a full propulsion-system test and that it worked.
4. That later payoff should explain why no later full sails / full engines test is required.

## Beat 11 — Cut Before Carnage

1. Do not show the wave impact.
2. Cut on the impossible upward motion.
3. Let the screams remain behind them.
4. Shift into `01_04 — The Cities That Disobeyed`.
