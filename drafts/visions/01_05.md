# 01_05 — José and Noel at Nikolai Headquarters

## Status

L4 vision reconstruction draft.

## Placement

Book 01 standalone vision sequence. Exact chapter insertion point TBD.

This is intended as **Vision 5**.

## Core Scene

José Mateo Navarro and Noel are alone in Noel's office at Nikolai headquarters.

## Setting

Nikolai headquarters, New York City.

- Top floor of a very expensive office building.
- The entire floor is Noel's office.
- Windows on every wall.
- Views of New York City in every direction.
- The office should feel excessive, insulated, and above the world it is deciding to abandon.
- Noel sits behind the desk.
- José sits across from him, visibly upset and increasingly frightened.

## Vision Function

This vision reveals that José's future betrayal / abandonment did not begin as grand ideology. It began as coercion, humiliation, fear, and a seat-count transaction.

Noel strips José of promised survival, then offers it back only if José helps him:

1. destabilize Hans and Frank's marriage;
2. arrange for Frank to be seduced by an escort at a specific date and time;
3. keep Hans / Frank from interfering with Noel's future plans;
4. sabotage transport capacity, delays, and civilian rescue logistics;
5. keep the entire bargain secret.

The scene should make José morally culpable without pretending Noel did not exploit terror and desperation.

## Character Positions

| Character | Position In Scene |
|---|---|
| Noel | Calm, wealthy, superior, coldly transactional, already treating survival seats as inventory. |
| José | Panicked, betrayed, humiliated, crying by the end, still willing to do terrible things to save himself and his family. |

# Beat-By-Beat Draft

## Beat 1 — The Firing

1. José sits across from Noel in the huge office.
2. He is clearly upset before the scene begins.
3. He understands enough to know what Noel is doing, but he needs to hear it.
4. José says:

> "You're firing me now?"

5. Noel answers without guilt:

> "Yes. You've already set this up. I no longer need an assistant."

6. Noel says he has people of actual value to take with him.
7. Noel is taking José's seats.
8. Noel is firing José.
9. The city outside should keep moving behind the glass, indifferent.

## Beat 2 — José Asks About His Family

1. José asks about himself and his family.
2. He asks whether Noel can at least get them into some lottery system.
3. He argues that he helped put this together.
4. Noel says no.
5. Noel repeats that José brings no value to the new group.
6. Noel asks:

> "What value can you offer me?"

7. José cannot believe it.
8. José calls Noel an asshole.
9. José says he did unspeakable things for Noel.
10. He says he handled the things Noel did not want to get his hands dirty with directly.

## Beat 3 — Intermediary / Feeder Logic

1. Noel acknowledges José's usefulness without gratitude.
2. He says José was the intermediary.
3. Noel says he no longer needs an intermediary.
4. Noel says José and his family would be feeders.
5. Required Noel idea:

> "I allowed you to come because you were useful. This is just efficiency at work."

6. The phrase should be monstrous because Noel believes it is rational.
7. José starts crying.
8. José gets up to leave.

## Beat 4 — Noel Stops Him

1. Noel insults José as he moves to leave.
2. Required Noel idea:

> "See, this is why you're pathetic. You do not even try to negotiate. You simply want to cry and walk away."

3. Noel says there is one thing José can do.
4. If José does it, Noel will give José and his family their seats back.
5. José knows there is a catch.
6. José answers bitterly:

> "Of course there is one thing. What would that be?"

## Beat 5 — Frank Must Be Seduced

1. Noel tells José he needs Hans and Frank to have marriage problems.
2. José must find an escort to seduce Frank.
3. Frank is vulnerable because he is unhappy with Hans being so consumed by the project.
4. It must happen on a specific date and at a specific time.
5. José asks why Noel is being so specific.
6. Noel says Hans / Frank will create problems for Noel's future plans if not redirected.
7. Required Noel idea:

> "I need him out of the way. He is going to create problems for future plans I have."

8. Noel's wording should be careful enough that José understands danger without getting the full plan.

## Beat 6 — Non-Breeders / Species Rescue Logic

1. Noel broadens the argument into species survival.
2. He frames the project as rescuing the species.
3. Noel asks whether, if they are rescuing the species, they really need to take any non-breeders with them.
4. Required Noel idea:

> "If we are rescuing the species, what value would they really offer?"

5. This should be ugly and eugenic in tone.
6. José should be disturbed, but he does not refuse.
7. José agrees to arrange the seduction.

## Beat 7 — Transport Sabotage

1. Noel adds another condition.
2. José is not to bring extra civilians aboard.
3. Noel needs more space.
4. José must interfere with transports, delays, shipments, and logistics if necessary.
5. Required Noel idea:

> "No extra civilians. I need more space. Delay transports, misroute shipments, create inefficiencies. Whatever language helps you sleep."

6. Noel may later call on José to help persuade the others.
7. José must not tell anyone.
8. If José does all this, his spaces are guaranteed.
9. José does not look happy.
10. José nods.
