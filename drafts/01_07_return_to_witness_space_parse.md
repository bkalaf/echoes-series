6. Rhün escorts Addie to Mae’s estate in Aquila Matara.

# Function

This section turns the two visions into Addie’s first actionable Book 1 objective.

It should:

- return Addie from historical-symbolic vision into the scene between stories;
- isolate Addie with Silence and Horror / Regret;
- establish that the other Witnesses have withdrawn;
- make Horror the immediate Witness obstacle;
- name the three candidate threads;
- give the riddle exactly enough shape to drive the first investigation;
- preserve yellow-beacon conditionality;
- return Addie to ordinary time with uncertainty intact.

The section is not an explanation scene. It is a tasking scene.

# Required Cast

- **Adeshka “Addie” H. Sonntag** — close-third POV.
- **Silence / SIL-3NCE** — guide, correction voice, evasive coach.
- **Horror / Regret** — first Witness obstacle; speaks in fragmented, measured cadence.

Optional / absent handling:

- The other Witnesses should be gone or visually absent by this point.
- Their absence should make Horror feel like the remaining gate rather than one voice in a crowd.

# Setting

## SET-WITNESS-VOID

The scene between stories / Witness-space after the mirrors.

The space should feel completed or emptied rather than merely dark:

- the prior crowd of Witnesses has withdrawn;
- the chamber/void holds sound differently;
- Horror remains as the only active obstacle;
- Silence is present but does not rescue Addie from ambiguity;
- the emotional residue of both mirrors is still active in Addie’s body.

# Beat Sequence

## Beat 1 — Return From Second Mirror

The Second Mirror dissolves.

Addie returns to the scene between stories carrying:

- the wrong-sky council fragments;
- Dianne’s vow / first alignment pressure;
- the First Dovren throne room;
- rainbow arch / three descending coins;
- the Overseer’s line: “That should survive, even if the people don’t.”

She should not have time to fully digest these before Horror begins.

## Beat 2 — Only Horror Remains

Addie notices the witness-space has changed.

The other Witnesses are gone.

Horror / Regret remains.

This is the moment Addie realizes the first Witness obstacle is no longer theoretical.

Do not let Addie already know the riddle or the three threads before Horror names them.

## Beat 3 — Horror Names The Three Threads

Horror names the three candidate threads:

1. **The Red Thread of Fate**.
2. **Ariadne’s Thread**.
3. **The Thread of the Norns**.

Silence may correct pronunciation or cadence if Addie fumbles **Norns**.

Addie should file each one in the “back room” as it arrives, but do not over-explain her memory mechanics.

## Beat 4 — Addie Tests The Shape Of The Task

Addie tries to convert the symbolic list into a practical task.

She may ask whether these are:

- objects;
- myths;
- metaphors;
- real historical artifacts;
- story-links;
- rewards;
- clues;
- traps.

Silence and Horror should not explain the whole system.

Silence can allow that stories are connective tissue, but not solve the choice for Addie.

## Beat 5 — Horror Gives The Riddle

Horror delivers the riddle in fragmented cadence:

> “Bring.”
>
> “The thread.”
>
> “That makes.”
>
> “The horned dead.”
>
> “Charge.”

The riddle should land as complete and insufficient.

Addie should recognize that the lack of explanation is part of the test, not an accidental omission.

## Beat 6 — Addie Connects The Mirrors Without Solving Them

Addie thinks back through:

- council chamber;
- Dianne’s vow;
- the First Dovren throne room;
- rainbow arch / three coins;
- the Bountiful Dam / river-diversion logic;
- the Overseer’s exact sentence.

She understands that symbols can carry hidden history and that a “thread” may be a causal path through story, not merely a physical cord.

She does **not** solve the correct thread yet.

## Beat 7 — Yellow Beacon Conditionality

Horror refuses further explanation.

He indicates the next threshold:

> “We shall see.”
>
> “When.”
>
> “And if.”
>
> “The beacon.”
>
> “Turns yellow.”

The yellow beacon should feel conditional, not guaranteed.

## Beat 8 — Return To Ordinary Time

The Witness-space dissolves.

Addie returns to the High Assembly / Conclave chamber.

This leads directly into the Rhün escort sequence:

- she has been gone approximately two hours;
- Rhün has waited by the bronze doors;
- blue beacon still burns;
- green beacon is expected tomorrow night;
- Rhün escorts her through Aquila Matara to Mae’s estate.

# Addie Handling

Addie should be overwhelmed but functional.

She can swear softly at the riddle because the riddle is structurally infuriating.

She should not become passive. Her active work here is:

- filing the three thread names;
- testing categories;
- noticing what Horror refuses to say;
- connecting the mirrors to symbol logic;
- understanding that the task is actionable even if the meaning is opaque.

Use “back room” imagery sparingly:

- shelves for thread names;
- a filed phrase for “That should survive”;
- a separate place for the rainbow arch / three coins.

Do not explain eidetic memory directly.

---

# Silence Handling

Silence should remain ambiguous and non-validating.

Allowed:

- pronunciation correction for “Norns”;
- sideways prompts;
- small clarifying pressure about stories as connective tissue;
- silence when Addie wants reassurance.

Avoid:

- “yes” validation;
- nodding approval;
- explaining which thread is right;
- explaining the reward system too clearly;
- explaining Horror’s full regret.

Possible Silence lines:

- “Stories are connective tissue.”
- “Past, present, future.”
- “The object matters because of what it teaches you to see.”
- “Curious, isn’t it?”
- “What would you ask yourself?”

---

# Horror / Regret Handling

Horror is not chatty.

He should speak with:

- fragmented cadence;
- pauses that feel chosen;
- the sense that the words are being dragged through something old and damaged;
- no comfort;
- no unnecessary explanation.

The later Witness lisp is an explicit phonetic exception, but this Book 1 riddle sequence should stay readable. Use physical cadence first; only use lisp if the current prose pass specifically calls for it.

Required exact phrases unless explicitly revised later:

- **“Bring the thread that makes the horned dead charge.”**
- **“We shall see. When. And if. The beacon. Turns yellow.”**
- **“Best of luck.”**
