# Hard Rule

Book 1 reveals only the surface layer:

- bells everywhere / nearly everywhere;
- bells disabled, tied down, wrapped, hidden, declappered, muffled, or decorated around;
- Mae refuses bell-summoning;
- Max laughs when the bells are mentioned;
- Mae shuts down the deeper story;
- Mae and Max share one unexplained look during Barnaby's Ariadne / moving-maze story.

Book 1 does **not** reveal:

- satyr blood magic;
- the bloodline binding;
- Mae discovering she is adopted;
- Dovren's blood contract;
- Max's mother's death;
- Max being trapped and starving;
- Max's Vayne descent;
- Geoffrik's locked book;
- the house creating rooms / trapping enemies.

# Chapter Insert Map

## `chapters/01_08_the_weight_of_being_asked.md`

### Insert A — Max formal name

At Max's first appearance, establish:

> “Maximilian T. Bellwether,” he said, with the kind of bow that made mockery and manners look like cousins. “Everyone who survives Lady Mae long enough calls me Max.”

Use exact wording only if it fits existing voice; otherwise preserve the content.

### Insert B — first muted bell seed

During Addie's first movement through Mae's foyer / corridor / guest threshold, insert one small observation:

- a little service bell on a table or beside a doorway;
- wrapped in dark cloth, tied down, hidden behind decorative filigree, or missing its clapper;
- Addie notices it but has no bandwidth to ask.

Suggested line shape:

> A small brass service bell sat on the side table, its clapper tied in black thread and its rim wrapped in velvet. Addie filed it as odd and kept walking.

Do not explain.

## `chapters/01_09_day_one_architecture.md`

### Insert C — repeated bell pattern

During Addie's morning bath / dressing / dining-room movement, add two or three quick sightings:

- a bell in the bath suite, wrapped in cloth;
- another near the dining sideboard, declappered;
- another in the corridor, worked into decoration but tied down.

Addie should realize this is a pattern.

Suggested line shape:

> The bath had one too: another little bell, hidden beneath a strip of embroidered cloth and tied down so tightly it could not have rung if the house had caught fire. By breakfast, Addie had seen three of them. That made it policy, not damage.

Do not explain.

## `chapters/01_12_the_unwound_core.md`

### Insert D — Max as household intelligence

Where Max appears or where household food / room preparation happens, strengthen him as the house's organizing intelligence.

He should not feel like background staff. He should be quietly moving food, staff, timing, and practical readiness around Mae's chaos.

Optional bell seed:

> There was another muted bell beside the family-room hearth, half hidden behind a vase of black reeds.

Do not explain.

Addie may notice and file it away:

Do not explain.

## `chapters/01_21_the_stupid_thread.md`

### Insert F — sandwich lunch / Max standards

When Max serves sandwiches after the group returns to the manor:

- narration may call him Max;
- he should be offended by the collapse of household standards, not by the companions personally;
- do not reveal bell lore.

Suggested line shape:

> Max served sandwiches with the expression of a man presenting battlefield casualties. “I had not planned for apocalypse lunch,” he said. “This is not our standard.”

If Mae addresses him, use:

- Bellwether if annoyed;
- Maximilian if threateningly polite;
- Max only if urgent or sincere.

## `chapters/01_22_go_with_the_eagle.md`

### Insert G — bell explanation

In the opening manor/family-pressure section, after the group friction but before departure, add the bell explanation beat.

Required shape:

1. Matthieu notices the disabled bell pattern.
2. Carrio identifies the social function: bells for summoning servants.
3. Max starts laughing.
4. Mae says: **“Don’t get him started.”**
5. Mae gives only the surface explanation:
   - her family used bells to call servants;
   - she stopped it when she took over the house;
   - she took over because she was sent to work with the Conclave, basically exiled from Nox Vaelor without the courtesy of anyone calling it exile.
6. Addie files it as another household scar.

Do not let anyone ask the full “why not remove them?” question in Book 1.

### Insert H — Max cusses Mae out / names matter

When Max calls out the room and specifically includes Mae:

- make it clear he can speak to her this way because he is not merely staff;
- he has history and permission earned through pain;
- Mae can take anger out on him because he is safe enough to take it;
- Max refuses to let her hide behind rank, old wounds, or sulking.

Mae's reply should use:

- **Bellwether** if annoyed;
- **Maximilian** if threateningly polite;
- **Max** only if sincere/urgent.

Preferred line shape:

> “Well said, Moderator,” Max said. “Said it much nicer than me, you asshats. And I mean you, Mae.”

Mae response options:

> “Bellwether.”

or

> “Maximilian, I am deciding whether to thank you or skin you.”

Do not let her casually call him Max in this beat unless the scene turns emotionally sincere.
