## `drafts/01_22_go_with_the_eagle.md`

### Max scolds the room

This is the key Book 1 use of the new Max/Mae dynamic.

Current beats already have Max entering after Addie's accidental-family speech, calling the group asshats, including Mae, and saying he has words for her if they survive.

Revise future prose so:

1. Max is the only household figure who can speak to Mae that way.
2. Mae takes out anger on him because he is safe enough to take it.
3. He refuses to let her hide behind rank, old wounds, or sulking.
4. Mae's reply should use **Bellwether** if annoyed or **Maximilian** if threateningly polite.
5. Use **Max** only if the moment turns emotionally sincere or strategically urgent.

Suggested line-shape:

> "Well said, Moderator. Said it much nicer than me, you asshats. And I mean you, Mae."

Mae answer should not call him Max casually. Prefer:

> "Bellwether."

or

> "Maximilian, I am deciding whether to thank you or skin you."

### Bell explanation scene

This chapter is the best Book 1 place to finally name the muted bells.

Before the final departure, Matthieu and Carrio may both notice the old disabled bells and ask what the pattern means.

Required shape:

1. Matthieu notices the same disabled bell pattern across rooms and asks about it as a pattern problem.
2. Carrio notices the social function: bells for summoning servants.
3. Max starts laughing.
4. Mae says: **"Don't get him started."**
5. Mae explains, briefly, that her family used to use bells to call servants.
6. She says she stopped that process when she took over the house.
7. She may add that she only took this house because she was sent to work with the Conclave — exile from Nox Vaelor without the courtesy of being called exile.
8. Do not reveal the Bellwether-name origin yet.

This keeps the Book 1 seed visible while preserving the later memory/vision reveal.
