# Primary Structural Purpose

The yellow Witness sequence should make the readers understand that the founding history of Erzengel changed rapidly and unnaturally:

- the original twenty-four city-states were triads;
- each triad contained one Human city, one Mythos city, and one Beastfolk city;
- the original power centers were Human capitals;
- Dovren weaponized natural identity divisions and racism to split populations apart;
- some non-Human cities gained status by submitting quickly to Dovren;
- some Human cities were punished for defying him and lost regional capital status;
- the official modern structure is therefore not a neutral civic evolution but a political scar.

The sequence should deepen, not fully solve, the mystery of why Mother created the original one-Human / one-Mythos / one-Beastfolk triad structure in the first place.

The full pact / formal law of forgetting should wait for the last set of visions.

# Revision A — End Of Prior Catastrophe / Ship Vision

## Function

The prior vision should not cut away too early.

Before José gets back into the helicopter, the vision should show him actively giving orders through the black box he is holding.

## Required Beats

1. José stands near the evacuation / pier / port crisis point holding the black command box.
2. He gives clipped orders through it.
3. Two ships clear their moorings first and begin making for open water.
4. José orders the other two ships out.
5. The other two ships begin disconnecting and pulling away.
6. People are left behind on the pier / waterfront.
7. Do **not** show carnage.
8. Do show panic, abandonment, and the obvious scale of what is about to happen.
9. As José gets into the helicopter, he sees the wave.
10. Required line: **"Oh Jesus Christ. Get us in the air now."**
11. It is dusk. The companions cannot make out every detail of the wave, but they can see enough to understand that it is enormous.
12. The people on the pier start screaming and running.
13. The companions should have a shared holy-shit reaction. This is not a normal flood; this is city-killing scale.
14. The ships go full speed, apparently trying to ride up the face of the wave or climb through impossible force.
15. The visual should imply some kind of magic / propulsion effect that none of the companions fully understands.
16. From the companions' angle and the darkness, it looks like the ships are riding the wave up.
17. The deeper truth: the ships are actually going airborne to avoid being smashed.
18. Do **not** explicitly explain that in this vision.
19. Cut before the wave impact / carnage.
20. Shift from the ships apparently climbing the wave into the fourth vision.

## Later Payoff Note

In a later vision or Mother conversation, reveal that they did not need to run a full propulsion-system test afterward because the emergency forced the test here and the system worked.

Use this as a future engineering / ship-system continuity payoff:

- no need to test full sails or full engines later;
- the crisis already proved the full propulsion system under impossible conditions;
- the companions should not understand this during the wave vision.

# Revision B — Fourth Vision: Dovren Rewards Fealty And Punishes Disobedience

## Function

The fourth vision should be a civic-founding / regional-capital punishment vision.

It shows Dovren using the split from the original mixed triads into separate cities as both public congratulation and racist consolidation.

It should also clarify why some cities lost current capital status while others gained it.

## Canon City Lock For This Vision

The Jadefall triad is now:

- Original Capital / Human City: **Qinyue**.
- Current Capital / Mythos City: **Grendhollow**.
- Beastfolk City: **Nettleburrow**.

The vision should specifically support this change:

- Qinyue disobeyed Dovren and lost regional capital status.
- Grendhollow submitted / established fealty and was elevated to current capital.

## Dovren's Public Frame

Dovren publicly congratulates the founding of the new cities and the separation of peoples out of Human cities.

His language should be intentionally ugly, racist, and politically revealing:

- he refers to the separated peoples as **mongrels** and **half-breeds**;
- he frames the split as proof that Mother's blending plan failed;
- he speaks primarily to the Human cities that remain capitals;
- he rewards obedience and punishes disobedience.

Required idea / line:

> "I told her this plan of blending mongrels, half-breeds, and humans would not work. So much for all-knowing."

This should clearly reference Mother, but still leave enough mystery that the reader does not fully understand who / what he means yet.

## Cities Called Out

Dovren should specifically name the Human cities that refused to send representatives:

- **Khembal**;
- **Qinyue**;
- **Skjarnheim**.

These Human cities are punished for disobedience by losing regional capital status.

Dovren should then begrudgingly congratulate the representatives of the cities that benefit from the shift:

- **Luminthalas** — current capital replacing Khembal in Ascentia;
- **Grendhollow** — current capital replacing Qinyue in Jadefall;
- **Nox Vaelor** — current capital replacing Skjarnheim in Gloamwood.

## Political Mechanism

The punishment should be practical and brutal, not merely symbolic.

Losing regional capital status means:

- trade is redirected away from the punished Human city;
- administrative attention moves to the newly favored capital;
- civic prestige collapses;
- investment and official patronage dry up;
- the punished city is driven toward poverty;
- its population is forced into cheap labor and economic dependence;
- Dovren creates an example: disobey the Sovereign and your city is made poorer.

The purpose is to show that fealty to Dovren creates opportunity, while resistance creates starvation and humiliation.

## Currency Note

The national currency should be called the **dovren**.

This makes Dovren's narcissism material and everyday: people must spend money named after him.

Use lower-case **dovren** for the currency unless it begins a sentence.

## Fealty Language Change

Replace the earlier "Thank you, Daddy" style line with something more formal, deferential, and politically servile.

Recommended line shape:

> "You have our unyielding support, High Sovereign. Whatever you require, we stand ready."

The subtext should be begging for favor.

The representative is not simply grateful; they are publicly performing loyalty because loyalty is now economically rewarded.

## Mae / Van'Kareth Family Discomfort

This vision still needs to make Mae uncomfortable through the Nox Vaelor component.

The first Van'Kareth / early Van'Kareth representative can be present among the Nox Vaelor delegation.

Mae should recognize enough family shape / name / crest / mannerism to feel implicated without the scene needing to fully explain her lineage yet.

The point is not that Mae personally caused anything. The point is that her family benefited from an old structure she does not yet understand.

## Dovren's Strategic Reveal

Dovren is not merely racist in private. He is openly shaping the nation through racism, economic punishment, and city-status manipulation.

He uses existing identity divisions — Human, Mythos, Beastfolk — and turns them into political hierarchy.

Some Mythos / Beastfolk / non-Human groups respond quickly because fealty puts them in a better position than rival cities in their own triad.

This is the first hard sign that systemic racism in Erzengel was not accidental drift. It was fueled, rewarded, and formalized by Dovren.

# Revision C — Post-Vision Return / Our Manor Beat

## Placement

This beat should happen after the companions return from the vision sequence and are trying to process what they saw.

It should function as the emotional decompression and immediate strategic reset.

## Required Beats

1. Someone reacts to Mae using **our manor**.
2. They look at her and say: **"Our manor?"**
3. Mae answers sharply that not a word about this happens until they get back to the house.
4. Mae then confirms the pronoun:

> "Yes. Our manor. It is abundantly clear to me right now that we have no idea what we are doing, or what our history really is, if what we just saw is real. Yes, our manor. This is so much bigger than us. My misplaced pronoun is probably the least of our fucking worries."

5. Gregory immediately starts hurling.
6. Everyone looks down at him.
7. Rhün pats Gregory's back.
8. Addie pulls out an H&H for him.
9. Mae takes the words / confirms the obvious:

> "Yes. It was that goddamn bad."

## Emotional Function

This beat should show that the visions have overwhelmed even the most controlled members of the group.

Mae's accidental **our manor** matters because it signals that she has started treating the companion group as a household / shared operational unit, not guests in her private estate.

But she is right: compared with what they just saw, the pronoun is a small issue.

Gregory vomiting is not a joke-only beat. It should be darkly funny, but it confirms the moral scale of the vision.

# Reader Takeaway

The reader should leave this sequence with these ideas:

1. The twenty-four original city-states were not simple modern capitals.
2. The founding structure seems to have been one Human / one Mythos / one Beastfolk city per regional pocket.
3. For some unknown reason, the system split rapidly.
4. Natural divisions by type were intensified by racism and political reward.
5. Dovren fueled the split and profited from it.
6. Disobedient Human cities such as Khembal, Qinyue, and Skjarnheim were punished by losing current capital status.
7. Luminthalas, Grendhollow, and Nox Vaelor rose partly because they submitted / participated / benefited at the right moment.
8. The later formal pact / forgetting law has not yet fully surfaced; save that for the final set of visions.
9. The rapid movement from triads into the modern civic structure should feel wrong. It happened too fast, too neatly, and with too much political advantage to be natural.
