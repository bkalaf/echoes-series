This chapter gives Addie her first direct experience of the hidden history beneath Eidolon without letting Book 1 reveal the whole architecture. The return sequence gives Addie her first Book 1 objective: identify the thread that makes the horned dead charge.

The first mirror is not standard scene witnessing. It is obstructed perception.

The second mirror is clearer because it reveals Book 1-appropriate political and symbolic material rather than full series-scale truth.

5. Above each chair is a colored name-flame / glass housing with a name and role.

2. The words vanish under a submerged roar / skull pressure / deep-water pressure effect.
3. Addie sees José speaking but cannot hear what he says.
4. The room’s reaction is clear: shock, fear, outrage, grief, and impossible calculation.
5. Addie tries to ask what is happening.
6. Silence says “baby steps” or similar.
7. Silence tells Addie to watch faces and remember how people look.
8. Addie protests that she needs words.
9. Silence answers that Addie has studied people, systems, sociology, emotion, and power; words are not the only evidence.
10. Addie forces herself to stop chasing blocked vocabulary.
11. She reads Seraphine’s horror, Hans’s restraint, Magnus’s protectiveness, Solomon’s spiritual collapse, José’s desperation, Dianne’s stillness, and Noel’s satisfaction.
12. Addie realizes the plan has failed or proven insufficient.
13. The room erupts without sound.
14. Seraphine slams hands on the table.
15. Magnus strikes the table.
16. Solomon clutches something to his chest.
17. José looks like a man who has said the thing he most feared saying.
18. Dianne remains calm, but the calm looks paid for.
19. Noel watches like someone measuring how much leverage has just entered the room.
20. Addie understands the emotional shape: the people in this room are deciding what humanity is allowed to become after catastrophe.
21. She does not know the forbidden nouns.
22. She knows enough to be afraid.
