# Beat-By-Beat Skeleton

## Subchapter 1 — After the White Light

1. The blinding white light fades from the tallow-district courtyard.
2. The peacekeepers have retreated toward the canal gates.
3. Their formation is broken; pikes are dropped or lowered; no one is eager to test Addie again.
4. Civilians remain hushed, shaken, and watching.
5. Upper-level scrying crystals / looking-glasses are cracked or dark after the light overload. Whether this damage is permanent remains a later decision.
6. Addie leans hard on her staff, physically drained.
7. Her cyan hair / braids still carry faint static from the moon discharge.
8. The pendant should feel cold, hot, or heavy, but do not over-explain its mechanism.
9. Mae looks controlled, but her stillness should read as dangerous restraint rather than calm.
10. Carrio remains partially coiled / hooded / venom-ready; if gum was present earlier, it is no longer casually moving.

## Subchapter 2 — The Woman in the Doorway

1. The restaurant door opens again.
2. Roslin appears first, gripping the frame hard enough to whiten her knuckles.
3. Brynn steps out behind or beside her, soot- and grease-marked from kitchen work.
4. If seeding Brynn’s signal, any faint work-song or hum from inside should cut off before she steps into the courtyard.
5. Brynn reads the scene in practical order: crowd, guards, Mae’s armor, Carrio’s body language, Addie, scroll.
6. She immediately assumes politics has brought violence to her doorstep.
7. Brynn’s first line should be an accusation, not awe.
8. Her accent can be present, but keep it controlled and readable.

## Subchapter 3 — Not From My Family

1. Brynn accuses Addie and company of being with “the lot of them” or otherwise connected to the garrison mess.
2. Addie says they are not with the guards and are trying to keep the guards out of Brynn’s business.
3. Addie clarifies the scroll is not from the Conclave in the ordinary bureaucratic sense and not a debt/family notice.
4. This matters because Brynn and Roslin believed the incoming scrolls were family harassment.
5. The crude middle-finger + Flying Scrolls sign should be retroactively clarified through tone or dialogue if needed.
6. Roslin asks Brynn what the scroll is and what Addie means.
7. Brynn does not answer quickly, showing she already suspects enough to hate it.
8. She mentions rumors: green beacon, people being tapped, powerful families / city fathers making messes and expecting others to fix them.

## Subchapter 4 — The Ninth Name

1. Addie says it is not a rumor; the scroll is proof.
2. Roslin, watching the neighborhood and understanding the stakes, tells Brynn she cannot refuse if this keeps people safe.
3. Roslin’s key function: she does not romanticize the calling; she tells Brynn to suck it up and do it because the neighborhood matters.
4. Brynn reaches for the scroll with lye-burned / work-worn fingers.
5. She cracks the green seal.
6. Green wax dissolves into floating embers or light.
7. Text realigns into: **Brynn H. Stonevein**.
8. Carrio notices the H and says something like: “H. Again.”
9. Addie feels relief and exhaustion together.
10. Addie understands this as the ninth name / end of the first sequence.
11. Do not turn this into a lore lecture. Let it land as pattern confirmation under time pressure.

## Subchapter 5 — A Life Left Behind

1. Brynn names what this costs her.
2. She spent roughly twenty years building a life away from Khaz Vorem.
3. She left inheritance, title, expectation, and high-born shadows behind.
4. She chose a kitchen and Roslin over dynastic machinery.
5. The scroll drags her back into the very pattern she fled.
6. Mae responds unusually gently, saying the spool found a way to pull Brynn back.
7. Addie observes the mismatch of lineages now gathered around her: dark elf princess, cobra beastfolk prince, dwarven chef, and herself.
8. The common thread is not public status; it is hidden inheritance, broken histories, and the unseen Mark / H-pattern structure.
9. Keep the Mark-of-the-Orbs lineage conversation for the next manor sequence. Do not solve it here.

## Subchapter 6 — Less Than an Hour

1. Addie checks the clock tower / beacon pressure.
2. The group has less than an hour before the green phase / portal-grid shutdown.
3. Addie asks if Brynn can leave the restaurant.
4. Brynn looks to Roslin before answering.
5. Roslin gives a single brave nod.
6. Brynn goes inside and returns quickly with a heavy leather satchel.
7. This should feel like someone who always had an emergency bag, whether she admits it or not.
8. Brynn says she can leave.
9. Her decision is grim, not eager.

## Subchapter 7 — The Corridor They Made

1. The lower-quarter crowd does not cheer wildly.
2. They step back and make a silent corridor.
3. This mirrors the earlier silent applause but should feel more solemn now.
4. They are not worshiping Addie; they are acknowledging danger and giving the group room to move.
5. Addie feels the weight of the scroll and the crowd’s silence.
6. Addie realizes the Overseer knew they would find Brynn here.
7. Carrio’s hood relaxes as they move, but his court mask is not fully back.
8. Carrio notes Brynn was the ninth name, the one held for “necessity.”
9. Addie suspects the next name / next stage is coming, but there is no time to process it.
10. The restaurant door closes behind them.
11. The tallow district begins to return to normal, but the silence left behind feels changed.
12. End with the group moving toward the terminal / manor-return sequence.
