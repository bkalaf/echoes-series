## Expand Team Two — Gregory / Brickett / Rhün

The White Tower Archives sequence must be expanded.

Core direction:

- Gregory should be dismissive at first and insist they return to the research.
- Gregory may concede the narrow point: folklore matters too, that is fair.
- But he must end the exchange by pushing back: that is **not** what they are researching now.

Important: do **not** let the chapter surface the full alternative idea yet. The scene should end with pressure against Gregory’s formal-research instincts, not with the solution.

## Expand Team One — Matthieu / Yurislav / Major Tallandra

The Aes Sidhal scene must also feel like active investigation.

Core direction:

- Matthieu should be the one asking clean, precise research questions.
- He should doodle / diagram on napkin, scrap paper, or ledger margin while interrogating the record problem.
- Yurislav should be emotionally keyed to the idea that people can be erased from records and that a symbol can outlive people.
- Tallandra should watch the clerk, exits, and threat vectors rather than merely stand there.

- Tallandra can ask the most practical question: who profited, who paid, who disappeared, or who stopped recording?
- Do not solve it yet. The trail remains dark, but the team should come away with frustration and a reason to keep digging.

2. A harmless mechanical / civic object swings or lurches toward Addie.
3. Brickett mistakes it for an attack.

5. He hits Addie in the stomach / ribs or is intercepted in a way that still creates a comic physical beat.

1. Team Three reaches Luminthalas and descends into the lower quarters.
2. The wealthy high-elf skybridges block direct sunlight, making high noon feel like twilight.
3. Addie pulls her tailored tunic tighter against the cold.
4. She asks how to handle Brynn when they find her.
5. Mae advises observing baseline and using values as leverage.
6. Carrio scans the alleys and storefronts; he is chewing gum casually.
7. The restaurant is hard to find because the lower quarters are a maze of soot, poor signage, and narrow courtyards.

9. The sign / restaurant name appears. Flag the naming issue: prior canon expects Bountiful Harvest; source text says Flying Scrolls. Do not resolve silently during parse.

## Subchapter 4 — The Line That Belongs To Them

1. Mae moves to cut the line.
2. Addie blocks her with the ironwood staff.
3. Addie explains that in poor districts, a place in line may be the only power someone has.
4. Addie says cutting the line would make them wait longer because people will defend their place just to prove they can.
5. Carrio accepts Addie’s experience despite admitting patience is not a family virtue.
6. They wait.
7. Their cover fails because Addie’s cyan hair / field clothes / public speech have made her recognizable.
8. Mae laughs at Addie’s assumption that the crowd is staring at noble jewelry.
9. Carrio points out Addie has permanently lost privacy.
10. Drunken highborn nobles harass Addie about the towel broadcast.
11. Mae and Carrio respond instantly and too violently.
12. Addie realizes they have caused a noble scene within minutes of arrival.

## Subchapter 5 — The White Tower Archives

1. Cut to Team Two after several hours in the White Tower Archives.
2. Gregory has turned through dozens of ancestral logbooks / legal records / thread myth indexes and found nothing useful.
3. Gregory adjusts unnecessary spectacles and fidgets with his bracelet.
4. Brickett has built a fort from priceless dictionaries and is eating in the reference area.
5. Rhün is restless, quiet, and bored by official records.
6. Gregory scolds both of them for treating the archive like a kitchen / animal-yard / fire-drake kennel.
7. Brickett says they have no starting coordinate and the books are the wrong container for the question.
8. Rhün suggests that handlers, fighters, stable songs, field stories, or ugly old folklore may carry what polished records exclude.
9. Gregory rejects the pivot and says they were assigned to research, not chase tavern songs.
10. Brickett says there has to be another way to look at it.
11. Rhün agrees, but does not solve it.
12. Gregory grudgingly says folklore matters too, that is fair.
13. Brickett brightens.
14. Gregory immediately shuts it down: that is not what they are researching now.
15. End with Gregory returning to the formal archive stack while Brickett/Rhün remain unconvinced.

## Subchapter 6 — The Dead Dam

1. Cut to Team One at Aes Sidhal / Satyr Arcane Academy.
2. Matthieu questions the chief archivist carefully, doodling a route / river / causal diagram while he speaks.
3. The clerk says there are zero useful logs on Bountiful Dam because it was a failed infrastructure project.
4. Details: first years of Dovren rule, cracked masonry, failed monsoon cycle, drained reservoir, abandoned sector, wild elementals, no successful civic outcome.
5. Yurislav’s coin goes over his knuckles until the clerk treats the erasure as ordinary.
6. Yurislav slams the counter and demands to know why a symbol would endure if the people and project did not.
7. Tallandra watches exits and the clerk’s body language, then asks practical record questions: who paid, who profited, who ordered abandonment, who stopped recording.
8. The clerk insists they are looking for ghosts.
9. Matthieu recognizes the clerk is likely not lying about the available books.
10. Matthieu also recognizes that absence of archive records may itself be a data point.
11. Do not solve it. The trail remains dark, but the team has a research pressure point.

## Subchapter 7 — Three Hours In The Cold

1. Return to Team Three in the line after three hours.
2. Addie calculates the remaining time to green phase.
3. The lower-quarter restaurant line remains slow but socially coherent.
4. The group has stripped / borrowed / stolen a cloak from the noble to hide Addie’s cyan hair from upper lenses.
5. Carrio leans near a stone support, chewing gum, scanning the kitchen through the window.
6. He sees the dwarf navigating tables with a heavy signet ring.
7. Carrio goes rigid; the gum stops moving.
8. He tries to push through the line toward the window.
9. Addie and Mae physically stop him.
10. The High Citadel bell tolls the Hour of the Aurora.
11. Concord / city peacekeepers begin district lockdown.

## Subchapter 8 — The Mark In The Kitchen

1. Carrio explains that the dwarf’s signet ring bears the same design as his family earrings.
2. Addie realizes the Mark may not be just about herself and Brickett.
3. Addie pulls out her simple silver pendant necklace.
4. Carrio removes an earring.
5. The pendant and earring match exactly.
6. Addie recalls Brickett’s backpack clasp also matches.
7. Addie and Carrio ask Mae where hers is.
8. Mae says she has never seen the symbol before and her family has no record of it.
9. Mae’s composure cracks; for once, she looks vulnerable.
10. Addie says there are too many threads and she does not know which one to pull.
11. Mae catches the accidental “threads” wording.
12. They do not resolve it before the street crisis escalates.

## Subchapter 9 — Chief Moderator In The Tallow-Tier

1. The garrison/peacekeepers move to shut down the street before the line gets served.
2. Mae chooses exposure over delay.
3. Mae tears away Addie’s cloak, revealing her cyan braids and field armor.
4. Mae publicly names herself, Carrio, and Addie.
5. Addie expects riot / anger / resentment.
6. Instead, the lower-quarter crowd recognizes Addie from the broadcast.
7. The crowd parts and offers silent applause.
8. Workers explain they did not realize it was the Head Moderator.
9. The door opens; Rosalind / Roslin appears first and panics at the scroll.
10. Brynn appears from the kitchen/scullery, covered in soot and grease, asking what they want with her.

## Subchapter 10 — White Lunar Light

1. Before Addie can explain, the peacekeepers enforce lockdown.
2. Mae steps into the phalanx and invokes Speaker authority.
3. The commander says the Conclave has no power right now.
4. Mae prepares lethal magic / Venom Blade response.
5. Carrio’s hood flares and poison response begins.
6. Addie sees the civilians caught in the middle.
7. Addie realizes a fight here ruins everything before day one is over.
8. Addie stops fighting the power attached to the pendant / Mark.
9. White lunar light erupts from her body / pendant / Mark pressure.
10. The courtyard goes white; peacekeepers and civilians drop back blinded.
11. Addie commands everyone to stay calm or she will ensure they cannot see at all.
12. End on the command / cliffhanger into the next chapter.
