# Interlude: The Sabotage of the Ledger

Dear Reader, I must apologize for interrupting again. I know how comforting it is to remain down there, wrapped safely inside the pretend world where the Sun People bicker with Apathetic Patriots, and obsessive occultists light fires in the dark to see who turns into a ghost first. It is an orderly kind of theater, isn't it? Even at its most violent, it stays inside its tracks.

But we need to go back to the land of the Sun People, the Apathetic Patriots, and the occultists. We need to look closely at how the occultists managed to leverage the power of the state, particularly in the Concord territories, using and weaponizing states that had meticulous records.

In this part of the world, lineage mattered; your family name meant a lot more than it really should. The occultists managed to weaponize that history and use it against minority populations—people who had signed up for registration cards twenty years prior, never thinking that their past obeisance to a law written before the occultists took power would be used to track them down and hunt their families almost to the point of extinction.

But sometimes minority populations find other minority populations—groups that normally do not agree on anything. In this case, the occultists also had a problem with who you loved. But let us be clear and call it for what it is: the occultists cared about replacement soldiers and population; they cared about young men to breed their war machine, and they disguised that as moral clarity. They leveraged one group of people who do not normally produce children, and that was homosexuals. Now, they are harder to identify; their masks slip every now and then, but they can generally stay hidden in plain sight.

And a certain group of these people did exactly that. They worked from the shadows and produced forged documents. People who found love in the arts discovered that art also makes for great forgeries. The same hand that creates beauty understands the precise technical replication of authority. They worked and they toiled, hand-crafting seventy thousand forged identity document packages for persecuted people. Working tirelessly over a number of years, they saved some seventy thousand people. That is a lot of people. That is a significant amount of people at the time, a city-sized buffer layer of survival comparable to the entire Jewish population of Amsterdam at the time—which stood at roughly seventy-eight thousand souls out of a total city population of eight hundred thousand.

They enabled people to try to live through the war by providing them an avenue where they would not get tracked down by a meticulous state machine. But they knew the risks going into this: you have to destroy the machine so the machine cannot reference the machine.

The authoritarian apparatus possessed a fatal check: the central registry repository. If a forged card's data was cross-referenced against the master folder and found to be a mismatch, the user was exposed to instant execution. Painting over the cracks was not enough; the master database itself had to be wiped from reality.

Think of the word *sabotage* and trace it back to its radical roots—the heavy wooden shoes, the *sabots*, thrown directly into industrial weaving looms by workers to snap the gears and halt an unyielding machine. It is fitting, then, to look at this occupied territory and call it the **Down-Low Countries**—the ultimate, defiant act of sabotage of the time, orchestrated by individuals forced into the shadows, using their invisibility to strip the gears of a genocidal registry.

They infiltrated the building disguised as the state's own law enforcement officers. To ensure zero innocent lives were lost, they drugged the guards, systematically flooded and piled the documents to maximize the incendiary fire damage, planted their devices, and blew the registry archive into ash. They shredded roughly eight hundred thousand baseline tracking records, throwing the tracking apparatus into complete and utter chaos.

The raid went off perfectly, without anyone caught on the night of the attack, but the structural betrayal came later via an informant. He took absolute responsibility for the bombing to protect his team, refusing to give up names. But the forces discovered their notebooks, leading to a show trial before a military tribunal where they were sentenced to death.

Before his execution by a firing squad on July 1, 1943, he left an uncompromised statement. We must enforce the strict, direct translation over the softened versions passed down by history, because his strict phrasing hits harder—it was a direct attack on the cold, definitive classification categories of the regime:

> **"Zeg de mensen dat homoseksuelen niet per definitie lafaards zijn."**
> *(“Tell the people that homosexuals are not by definition cowards.”)*

History is a fickle archivist; it forgot them almost immediately after the conflict ended. Society preferred comforting falsehoods to the bitter truth of who held their shield. Who would have thunk that the persecution of one group of these people would continue for another eighty years? The very legal right to marry would be stripped from them, and they would be kicked out of military service for not having enough "honor." The group of people who literally saved hundreds of thousands.

Conservative estimates would say that two hundred and seventy thousand people owe their lives directly to the actions of this handful of eleven, led by two homosexuals.

Think of the absolute, stinging irony of it, Dear Reader. The two people who biologically could not produce a single child ended up tying more human permanence, more continuous lineages, and more living names to their legacy than ninety-eight percent of the breeding populations on the planet.

Now let us return straight back to **Matthieu’s story** in Book 11, and look at how our own characters handle a ledger that has decided they should no longer exist.
